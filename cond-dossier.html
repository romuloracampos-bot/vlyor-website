Product Overview

CondiFlex 7 is Polyquaternium-7 — a cationic graft copolymer of acrylamide and diallyldimethylammonium chloride (DADMAC), the most commercially used conditioning polymer in rinse-off personal care globally by formulation frequency. Its high permanent positive charge density, derived from the quaternary ammonium groups on the DADMAC monomers, enables strong substantive deposition onto the negatively charged surface of damaged hair (from bleaching, heat or mechanical stress) and skin, surviving the rinse step to deliver conditioning benefit in the dry product.

The defining commercial advantage of PQ-7 is its compatibility with anionic surfactant systems. Most cationic actives form insoluble coacervates with anionic surfactants like SLES at formulation concentrations, causing precipitation and heterogeneous conditioning deposition. PQ-7's acrylamide content moderates charge density sufficiently to remain in solution within typical anionic surfactant cleansing bases — enabling direct incorporation into shampoos, body washes and conditioning cleansers without phase separation or precipitate formation.

Mechanism of Action — Coacervate Deposition

During application in a shampoo or cleansing product, PQ-7 exists in solution within the concentrated surfactant system at formulation concentration. As the product is diluted with rinse water, the surfactant concentration decreases below the critical micelle concentration, and the charge ratio between cationic polymer and anionic surfactant micelles shifts — causing formation of a coacervate (polymer-rich phase) that deposits onto the anionic sites of the hair surface. This dilution-triggered deposition mechanism ensures that the polymer delivers its conditioning benefit specifically during the rinse step, where contact with the hair surface is maximised. The deposited PQ-7 film reduces cuticle-to-cuticle friction during wet combing, reduces flyaway charge in the dry state and provides a smooth, silky tactile experience.

Product Overview

CondiFlex 10 is Polyquaternium-10 — cationic hydroxyethylcellulose produced by reacting hydroxyethylcellulose (HEC, a cellulose derivative) with a glycidyltrimethylammonium chloride quaternising agent. The cellulosic backbone provides structural rigidity and exceptional film-forming ability on hair fibres; the grafted quaternary ammonium groups provide substantive deposition onto the negatively charged hair surface through electrostatic attraction. The resulting polymer combines the film-forming architecture of cellulosics with the conditioning function of cationic polyelectrolytes.

PQ-10 is the preferred conditioning polymer for leave-on hair formulations — sprays, serums, masks, heat protectants and leave-in conditioners — where sustained conditioning benefit between washes is the primary product objective. The cellulosic film persists on the hair shaft between washes without the greasy build-up associated with high-MW dimethicone, maintaining combing ease and surface smoothness over multiple days. The natural-origin cellulose backbone also makes it compatible with COSMOS-certified natural formulations when sourced appropriately.

Mechanism of Action — Film Formation + Substantive Deposition

PQ-10's mechanism combines electrostatic and structural elements. The cationic quaternary ammonium groups adsorb electrostatically onto negatively charged hair surface sites — particularly concentrated at zones of cuticle damage (raised or missing cuticle scales). Once adsorbed, the cellulosic polymer backbone extends across the hair surface, forming a continuous flexible film that bridges multiple cuticle cells simultaneously. This film formation — absent in solution-state polymers like PQ-7 which deposit as discontinuous molecular layers — provides structural reinforcement that reduces cuticle cracking under combing tension, measurably improving fibre tensile strength and break resistance. Heat protection mechanism: the cellulosic polymer film provides a thermal barrier that reduces the heat flux reaching the cortex during blow-drying and styling, reducing thermal denaturation of cortical proteins at the same applied temperature. Maximum protection is achieved at 0.3–0.5% with heat protection sprays applied before styling.

Product Overview

CondiFlex 39 is Polyquaternium-39 — a high-molecular-weight cationic terpolymer of acrylamide, acrylic acid and diallyldimethylammonium chloride. This amphoteric polyelectrolyte structure — combining anionic carboxylate groups from acrylic acid with cationic quaternary ammonium groups from DADMAC in a single polymer chain — enables PQ-39 to function simultaneously as a conditioning agent, viscosity builder and suspension polymer in aqueous personal care systems.

PQ-39 is particularly valuable in conditioning shampoo formulations where viscosity is required alongside conditioning — providing both functions from a single polymer addition. Where a conventional formula might require a salt thickening system (NaCl/NaCitrate) plus a separate conditioning polymer (PQ-7), PQ-39 at 0.1–0.3% delivers both effects with greater salt-independence and across a wider pH range. Its amphoteric character also enables useful suspension properties for pearlescent agents and physical exfoliants.

Dual-Function Mechanism — Thickening + Conditioning

Thickening mechanism: PQ-39's high-MW polymer chains bridge between anionic surfactant micelles through electrostatic interaction between the DADMAC cationic groups and micelle surfaces, forming a transient polymer-surfactant network that increases macroscopic viscosity. Unlike salt thickening (which works by reducing electrostatic repulsion between charged micelles), PQ-39 thickening is effective across a broader pH range and is compatible with salt-free formulation briefs — increasingly important in premium and professional hair care where sodium chloride is avoided due to cuticle swelling concerns. Conditioning mechanism: the same cationic DADMAC groups that interact with surfactant micelles also interact with the negatively charged hair surface, with the coacervate-deposition mechanism analogous to PQ-7. The high-MW architecture and amphoteric character provide slightly different deposition kinetics from PQ-7, with PQ-39 tending to deposit more readily in moderate-surfactant-concentration environments typical of conditioning shampoos.

Product Overview

CondiFlex C16 is Cetrimonium Chloride (Hexadecyltrimethylammonium Chloride) — a long-chain quaternary ammonium salt supplied in two active concentration grades: CTAC-30 (≥30% active) and CTAC-50 (≥50% active). As a cationic surfactant with a C16 hexadecyl chain, it is the fundamental conditioning-active building block of personal care — the ingredient responsible for the adsorption of conditioning agents onto the hair shaft and the lamellar gel structuring of rinse-out conditioner emulsions.

CTAC differs commercially from CondiSoft BTMS in that it is supplied as the pure cationic active in aqueous solution, giving formulators independent control over active concentration, fatty alcohol ratio and emulsion structure. Combined with cetearyl alcohol (4–8%), CTAC at 1–3% creates the ternary surfactant/fatty alcohol/water lamellar system that is the structural foundation of every rinse-out conditioner — the thick, spreadable gel that deposits conditioning film during rinse-off. Both the 30% and 50% grades meet identical quality specifications for active content, pH (6–8), free amine (≤1.5%) and colour.

Mechanism of Action — Lamellar Gel Formation + Cationic Adsorption

Lamellar gel mechanism: In the ternary system (CTAC / fatty alcohol / water), the cationic surfactant headgroup and the fatty alcohol hydroxyl group form hydrogen-bonded mixed bilayers — interdigitated lamellar structures that create a gel-phase network throughout the aqueous conditioner. This lamellar structure is responsible for the characteristic viscosity, spreadability and sensory richness of conditioner products; it is also the delivery mechanism for conditioning deposition. As the conditioner dilutes with rinse water, the lamellar gel collapses, releasing CTAC cations that adsorb onto the negatively charged hair surface via electrostatic attraction. Cationic adsorption mechanism: The permanent positive charge of the trimethylammonium headgroup is attracted to the net-negative surface charge of damaged hair cuticle (from oxidative damage, mechanical stress or alkaline processing that removes the natural 18-MEA fatty acid surface layer). The C16 hexadecyl chain orients away from the surface, creating a monomolecular conditioning film that reduces cuticle-to-cuticle friction, reduces static charge build-up and imparts the characteristic post-conditioning softness and manageability. Antimicrobial contribution: At cosmetic use concentrations, CTAC provides mild antimicrobial activity against bacteria and fungi — an intrinsic property of quaternary ammonium compounds that contributes to the overall preservation efficiency of conditioner formulas, reducing the total preservative load required for ISO 11930 compliance.

Product Overview

CondiSoft BTMS is Behentrimonium Methosulfate (BTMS) at 25% in cetearyl alcohol — the definitive cationic emulsifier for rinse-out hair conditioners, deep conditioning masks and leave-in cream treatments. Behentrimonium methosulfate is a quaternary ammonium compound with a C22 (behenyl) fatty chain — the long alkyl chain provides the hydrophobic membrane insertion that anchors the conditioning molecule to the hair surface, while the permanent positive charge of the quaternary ammonium head provides electrostatic attraction to the negatively charged damaged hair cuticle.

BTMS's functional differentiation from non-ionic emulsifiers is fundamental to conditioner product architecture: a BTMS-emulsified O/W emulsion inherently deposits conditioning as the emulsion is diluted by rinse water. As the conditioner dilutes from concentrated product (~5–8% BTMS) to dilute rinse water, the cationic BTMS headgroups adsorb onto the anionic hair surface, leaving a substantive quaternary ammonium conditioning film — the source of the post-conditioning slip, softness and manageability that consumers experience. This combined emulsification and conditioning deposition in a single material is BTMS's commercial raison d'être.

The cetearyl alcohol co-component (75% of the blend) contributes essential lamellar liquid crystal structure to the conditioner emulsion — forming a bilayer arrangement with the BTMS that creates the thick, creamy, stable emulsion texture characteristic of premium conditioners and the rich sensory profile that consumers associate with intensive conditioning products.

Mechanism of Action — Cationic Emulsification + Rinse-Deposition

BTMS forms stable O/W emulsions through a combination of geometric packing and electrostatic stabilisation. The C22 behenyl chain provides a large hydrophobic tail that favours formation of lamellar (bilayer) structures with cetearyl alcohol; the quaternary ammonium headgroup protrudes into the aqueous phase, creating a positively charged emulsion surface that provides electrostatic stability against droplet coalescence at typical cosmetic pH (3–5). During rinse-out application, as the product dilutes with water, three simultaneous processes occur: (1) the emulsion destabilises as the lipid concentration falls below the critical lamellar concentration; (2) the BTMS cationic headgroups — no longer shielded within the emulsion structure — present directly to the anionic hair surface; (3) electrostatic adsorption deposits the BTMS molecule onto the hair cuticle, with the C22 behenyl chain oriented away from the surface, creating a monomolecular conditioning film. This film reduces cuticle friction and provides slip during wet combing and detangling, and softness and manageability in the dry state.

Product Overview

CondiVeil 949 is a pre-formulated conditioning emulsion combining Amodimethicone, Cetrimonium Chloride and Trideceth-12 — a precision delivery system providing highly substantive silicone conditioning with inherent anionic surfactant compatibility. Standard polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) fluids distribute non-selectively across the hair shaft and gradually build up on undamaged sections; CondiVeil 949 contains amodimethicone — an amino-functional polysiloxane where primary and secondary amine groups on the silicone backbone provide affinity for the high-density negative charge zones of damaged hair cuticle.

The damage-targeted deposition mechanism is amodimethicone's defining commercial property: silicone conditioning is delivered with highest intensity at the damaged cuticle zones — bleached, colour-treated or heat-damaged sections with the greatest negative charge density — rather than coating uniformly. This selectivity prevents build-up on undamaged hair sections while concentrating the conditioning benefit exactly where performance is most needed. The cetrimonium chloride and Trideceth-12 delivery vehicle enable dispersion and stability of the otherwise-insoluble silicone in both aqueous conditioner matrices and anionic surfactant 2-in-1 systems — a compatibility that standard amino silicone emulsions without this delivery system cannot achieve.

Mechanism of Action — Targeted Amino Silicone Deposition

Hair damage from chemical processing (bleaching, colouring, permanent waving) and mechanical/thermal stress creates high-density sites of negative charge on the cuticle surface — primarily from denatured or degraded surface-bound 18-MEA (18-methyleicosanoic acid) fatty acid layer and exposed anionic amino acid residues. Amodimethicone's primary amine groups (–NH₂) carry positive charge at the slightly acidic pH of conditioning products (3.5–5.0), enabling strong electrostatic interaction with these anionic damage sites. Once adsorbed, the silicone backbone — a flexible polysiloxane chain — spreads laterally across the cuticle surface, filling microscopic surface defects and creating a smooth, hydrophobic coating that reduces cuticle friction and repels atmospheric moisture. The reactive nature of the amine groups also enables formation of semi-covalent bonds with carboxylic and aldehydic groups on severely damaged hair, creating a more durable conditioning film than non-reactive silicones. This durability translates into better wash-fastness — CondiVeil 949-based conditioning survives more wash cycles than equivalent non-functional silicone treatments — and into sustained colour protection between salon visits (silicone film retards outward diffusion of dye molecules from the cortex).
