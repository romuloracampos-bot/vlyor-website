The Conditioning Argument

The feel consumers
cannot define but

Consumer satisfaction in hair and skin care is dominated by one variable above all others: tactile experience. Functional performance — cleaning efficacy, pH balance, preservation — is invisible. Sensory performance is what the consumer experiences, remembers and returns for. A conditioner that does not feel conditioning costs the brand its repurchase rate, regardless of what the formula technically delivers.

VLYOR COND addresses the chemistry behind sensory performance with precision. CondiFlex polyquaterniums deposit conditioning polymers from anionic surfactant bases — surviving the rinse step to smooth the cuticle and reduce combing friction. CondiSoft BTMS builds the cationic emulsifier matrix that gives rinse-out conditioners their signature rich texture and slip. CondiVeil 949 deposits reactive amino silicone at exact sites of hair damage — the high-charge zones where standard dimethicone provides no selectivity.

"The formulator who understands cationic conditioning chemistry understands why one product feels like water and another feels like a treatment. The difference is not concentration — it is selection."

CondiFlex 7

Polyquaternium-7

Cationic PolymerSkin & HairUniversalAnionic Compatible

"The most versatile conditioning polymer in personal care — conditioning both hair and skin across every rinse-off format from body wash to 2-in-1 shampoo, without precipitating in anionic surfactant systems."

CondiFlex 7 is Polyquaternium-7 — a cationic graft copolymer of acrylamide and diallyldimethylammonium chloride (DADMAC), the most commercially used conditioning polymer in rinse-off personal care globally. Its high permanent positive charge density — derived from the quaternary ammonium groups on the DADMAC monomers — enables strong substantive deposition onto the negatively charged surface of damaged hair (from bleaching, heat or mechanical trauma) and skin, with the cationic polymer surviving the rinse step to deliver conditioning benefits in the dry product.

The defining commercial advantage of PQ-7 is its compatibility with anionic surfactant systems. Most cationic conditioning actives form insoluble complexes (coacervates) with anionic surfactants like SLES, precipitating from the formulation and depositing a heterogeneous conditioning residue. PQ-7's acrylamide content moderates the charge density sufficiently to remain in solution within typical anionic surfactant cleansing bases — enabling direct incorporation into shampoos, body washes and conditioning cleansers without phase separation.

Performance Detail

In hair care, CondiFlex 7 delivers the complete rinse-off conditioning spectrum: detangling, wet and dry combing friction reduction, flyaway and static control, and a smooth, silky dry feel at concentrations of 0.3–0.8%. In skin care and body wash, the substantively deposited polymer film measurably reduces transepidermal water loss (TEWL) in repeated use applications and provides the characteristic post-wash softness associated with conditioning body washes. Its low viscosity solution form (typically 10% active in water) enables straightforward incorporation with no heating or pre-dissolution required.

CondiFlex 10

Polyquaternium-10

Cationic CelluloseFilm FormerLeave-On SpecialistNatural-Derived

"Cellulose-derived cationic conditioning polymer — outstanding film formation and substantivity for leave-on hair formulations, heat protection and sustained between-wash conditioning benefit."

CondiFlex 10 is Polyquaternium-10 — cationic hydroxyethylcellulose produced by reacting hydroxyethylcellulose (a cellulose derivative) with a quaternary ammonium reagent, combining the structural properties of natural cellulosic polymers with the substantive conditioning function of quaternary ammonium groups. The cellulosic backbone provides exceptional film-forming ability on hair fibres — creating a continuous, flexible polymer film across the cuticle surface that improves tensile strength, reduces breakage under combing and styling stress, and provides a degree of UV-absorbing protection to the underlying cortex.

PQ-10 is the preferred conditioning polymer for leave-on hair formulations — sprays, serums, masks, heat protectants — where sustained conditioning benefit between washes is the primary objective. The cellulosic film persists on the hair shaft between washes, maintaining combing ease and surface smoothness over multiple days without the build-up associated with high-MW silicones. Its dry powder form also simplifies formulation of conditioning hair powders and dry shampoo-conditioner hybrids.

Performance Detail

The film-forming character of CondiFlex 10 provides benefits that solution-state polymers like PQ-7 cannot: anti-breakage protection through tensile strength reinforcement of the hair fibre surface; heat protection through the thermal barrier function of the polymer film during blow-drying and heat-styling (effective up to approximately 180°C when combined with heat-activated actives); and styling hold without the rigidity of conventional fixative polymers. These properties make it the standard conditioning polymer for professional salon-grade leave-on products.

CondiFlex 39

Polyquaternium-39

Cationic TerpolymerConditioning + ThickeningAmphoteric

"The conditioning thickener — one high-MW polymer that simultaneously builds viscosity and deposits conditioning polymer, reducing the ingredient count in conditioning shampoo formulations."

CondiFlex 39 is Polyquaternium-39 — a high-molecular-weight cationic terpolymer of acrylamide, acrylic acid and diallyldimethylammonium chloride. This amphoteric polyelectrolyte architecture — combining anionic carboxylate groups from acrylic acid with cationic quaternary ammonium groups from DADMAC — enables PQ-39 to function simultaneously as a conditioning agent, viscosity builder and suspension polymer in personal care systems, providing multiple formulation functions from a single ingredient.

PQ-39's thickening mechanism operates through electrostatic interaction with anionic surfactant micelles in the formulation — the high-MW polymer bridges between micelles, increasing solution viscosity through polymer-surfactant network formation. Unlike salt thickening (NaCl in SLES systems) which narrows the acceptable pH and temperature ranges, PQ-39 thickening is salt-independent and pH-stable across the full range of typical surfactant product formulation.

High MWPolymer architecture

Performance Detail

The commercial value of CondiFlex 39 is ingredient count reduction in conditioning shampoo formulations. Where a conventional formulation might require a rheology modifier (carbomer, HPMC or salt thickening system) plus a separate conditioning polymer (PQ-7 or PQ-10), PQ-39 delivers both functions. This simplification reduces cost per formula, simplifies manufacturing (fewer additions, one dissolution step) and can contribute to a cleaner, shorter ingredient declaration — increasingly important in global premium market positioning.

CondiFlex C16

Cetrimonium Chloride

Cationic SurfactantConditioning AgentAntisepticTwo Grades

"The foundational cationic quaternary ammonium conditioner — Cetrimonium Chloride at 30% and 50% active, the building-block conditioning surfactant that forms the lamellar gel structure of hair conditioners in combination with fatty alcohols."

CondiFlex C16 is Cetrimonium Chloride (Hexadecyltrimethylammonium Chloride / CTAC) supplied in two grades: 30% and 50% active content — the fundamental cationic conditioning surfactant in personal care, providing the quaternary ammonium charge that is the basis of every conditioning mechanism from simple rinse-off softening to the lamellar liquid crystal structure of premium conditioner emulsions. Its C16 hexadecyl chain provides a precisely calibrated balance of hydrophobicity and water solubility for optimal adsorption onto the hair shaft and skin surface at cosmetic use concentrations.

Combined with fatty alcohols (cetyl alcohol, stearyl alcohol, cetearyl alcohol), CTAC forms the defining ternary system of rinse-out conditioners: the surfactant/fatty alcohol/water gel phase that creates the thick, stable lamellar structure responsible for the rich conditioner texture. This is the same fundamental chemistry behind CondiSoft BTMS, but supplied as the individual cationic surfactant component — giving formulators direct control over the balance between CTAC and fatty alcohol to tune viscosity, conditioning intensity and texture independently. At 1–3% CTAC + 3–6% cetearyl alcohol, a complete conditioning emulsion base is established.

CondiFlex C16 also delivers mild antimicrobial activity at cosmetic use concentrations — an intrinsic property of quaternary ammonium compounds that contributes to preservative system efficiency and provides a degree of scalp hygiene benefit in rinse-off conditioning products. Available in 30% grade for dilute application systems and easy incorporation, and 50% grade for high-active conditioner bases and cost-efficient high-concentration manufacturing.

Performance Detail

The 30% grade (CTAC-30) offers simpler handling and lower viscosity for aqueous incorporation, ideal for rinse-off conditioning emulsions and shampoo systems. The 50% grade (CTAC-50) provides higher active loading per volume — reducing transportation and storage costs per active unit — and is preferred for manufacturing high-concentration conditioning bases. Both grades conform to ≤1.5% free amine specification, ensuring minimal odour and high-purity active content.

CondiSoft BTMS

Behentrimonium Methosulfate (and) Cetearyl Alcohol

Emulsifying ConditionerCationic O/W EmulsifierCOSMOS-EligibleAll Hair Types

"The cationic emulsifier that makes rinse-out conditioners possible — BTMS 25% provides structural emulsification, rich sensory texture and substantive conditioning deposition in a single raw material."

CondiSoft BTMS is Behentrimonium Methosulfate at 25% in cetearyl alcohol — the definitive cationic emulsifier for rinse-out conditioners, deep conditioning masks and leave-in cream treatments. Behentrimonium methosulfate (BTMS) is a quaternary ammonium compound with a C22 (behenyl) fatty chain that provides a positively charged emulsifier matrix — unlike the neutral non-ionic emulsifiers used in skin care moisturisers, BTMS deposits a conditioning cationic film on hair fibres during the rinse-out process as the emulsion dilutes with water.

This simultaneous emulsification and conditioning deposition function is BTMS's fundamental commercial advantage: a single raw material provides the emulsifier, the conditioning active and the product structure — the thick, creamy, spreadable consistency that consumers identify with premium hair conditioning. The cetearyl alcohol co-component provides additional lamellar structure to the emulsion and contributes to the rich, heavy texture characteristic of deep conditioning masks.

BTMS is the universal conditioning emulsifier for natural, clean-label and professional hair care — COSMOS-eligible, widely used across certified natural conditioner formulations, and suitable for all hair textures with particular efficacy for high-porosity chemically treated, natural coily and kinky textures that require intensive conditioning.

Performance Detail

At 4–6% BTMS in a standard rinse-out conditioner, the lamellar liquid crystal structure formed by the BTMS/cetearyl alcohol matrix creates a stable O/W emulsion with exceptional spreadability and sensory profile. During rinse-off, dilution of the cationic emulsifier system promotes coacervate deposition — the BTMS cationic headgroup adsorbs onto the anionic hair surface as the counterion (methosulfate) dissipates in the dilute rinse water, leaving a persistent conditioning quaternary ammonium film on the hair shaft. This film reduces cuticle friction, prevents moisture loss and is responsible for the slip, softness and manageability of the conditioned hair.

CondiVeil 949

Amodimethicone (and) Cetrimonium Chloride (and) Trideceth-12

Silicone ConditionerTargeted DepositionFrizz ControlAnionic Compatible

"Reactive amino silicone with built-in deposition technology — targeted conditioning at damage sites for shine, frizz control and colour protection with anionic surfactant compatibility."

CondiVeil 949 is a pre-formulated conditioning emulsion combining Amodimethicone, Cetrimonium Chloride and Trideceth-12 — a precision delivery system that enables highly substantive silicone conditioning deposition on damaged hair while maintaining compatibility with anionic surfactant cleansing systems. Standard polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS/dimethicone) fluids deposit non-selectively across the hair shaft; CondiVeil 949 contains amodimethicone — an amino-functional reactive silicone in which amino groups on the silicone backbone interact specifically with the high-density negative charge zones of chemically or mechanically damaged hair cuticle.

This damage-targeted deposition mechanism is the commercial differentiator of amodimethicone versus conventional silicone fluids: the silicone is delivered precisely where the hair needs it most — bleached, colour-treated or heat-damaged cuticle zones — rather than coating undamaged sections and contributing to build-up. The cetrimonium chloride and Trideceth-12 components function as the delivery vehicle — enabling the otherwise-insoluble silicone to disperse in aqueous systems and maintain stability in the anionic surfactant environment of 2-in-1 shampoos without precipitating.

Performance Detail

The amino groups on amodimethicone's backbone also provide reactivity with the hair surface beyond simple electrostatic adsorption — the amine can form covalent or semi-covalent bonds with aldehydic and carboxylic groups on severely damaged hair, creating a more durable conditioning film than non-reactive dimethicone provides. This translates into measurably better colour protection (silicone film retards dye molecule diffusion out of the cortex), improved heat protection (silicone thermal barrier), and frizz control that persists through humidity exposure — all commercially valuable claims for premium hair care across global markets.
