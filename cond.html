Consumer satisfaction in hair and skin care products is dominated by one variable above all others: how the product feels on the skin and in the hair. Functional performance — cleaning efficacy, pH balance, preservation — is invisible to the consumer. Sensory performance is what they experience, remember and return for.

VLYOR COND addresses the chemistry behind sensory performance — the cationic polymers that deposit conditioning films during the rinse step, the BTMS matrix that creates the rich texture of premium conditioners, and the targeted amino silicone that delivers frizz control and shine at the precise sites of hair damage.

"The formulator who understands cationic conditioning chemistry understands why one shampoo feels like water and another feels like a treatment. COND provides the actives that enable the second category."

VLYOR COND — Conditioning Systems

CondiFlex 7

Polyquaternium-7

Cationic PolymerSkin & HairUniversal

"The most versatile conditioning polymer available — a single active that conditions both hair and skin across every rinse-off format."

CondiFlex 7 is Polyquaternium-7 — a cationic graft copolymer of acrylamide and diallyldimethylammonium chloride, the most widely used conditioning polymer in rinse-off personal care. Its high positive charge density enables strong substantive deposition onto the negatively charged surface of damaged hair and skin, surviving the rinse-off step to deliver conditioning benefits in the final dry product.

In hair care, CondiFlex 7 provides detangling, wet combability improvement, static reduction, flyaway control and a smooth, silky dry feel — the full spectrum of conditioning benefits expected from a premium rinse-off product. In skin care and body wash, it deposits a moisturising, slip-enhancing film that persists after rinsing, measurably reducing TEWL in repeat-use studies.

Its compatibility with anionic surfactant systems — the standard cleansing base — without precipitation or complex formation is the defining formulation advantage of PQ-7 over other cationic conditioning agents, which often require careful surfactant selection to avoid coacervation.

CondiFlex 10

Polyquaternium-10

Cationic CelluloseHair ConditioningFilm Former

"Cellulose-derived cationic polymer — exceptional substantivity and film formation for long-lasting hair conditioning."

CondiFlex 10 is Polyquaternium-10 — a cationic hydroxyethylcellulose produced by quaternisation of natural cellulose, combining the structural properties of cellulosic polymers with the substantive conditioning action of quaternary ammonium groups. The cellulosic backbone provides outstanding film-forming ability on hair fibres, creating a coherent polymer film that improves tensile strength, reduces breakage and provides UV protection to the hair shaft surface.

PQ-10 is the preferred conditioning polymer for leave-on hair formulations — sprays, masks, heat protectants and serums — where sustained conditioning benefit between washes is the primary product objective. Its film-forming properties also make it useful in spray-on styling products where hold and definition are required alongside conditioning.

CondiFlex 39

Polyquaternium-39

Cationic PolymerThickenerMulti-functional

"The conditioning thickener — body, viscosity and conditioning in a single high-MW polymer."

CondiFlex 39 is Polyquaternium-39 — a high-molecular-weight cationic terpolymer of acrylamide, acrylic acid and diallyldimethylammonium chloride. This amphoteric polyelectrolyte structure enables PQ-39 to function simultaneously as a cationic conditioning agent, viscosity builder and thickener in aqueous personal care systems — providing rheological benefits in addition to the substantive deposition conditioning characteristic of polyquaterniums.

PQ-39 is particularly valuable in conditioning shampoo formulations where viscosity is required alongside conditioning — reducing or eliminating the need for separate rheology modifiers while delivering both benefits from a single ingredient. Its ability to thicken anionic surfactant systems via electrostatic interaction with the polymer's anionic acrylic acid groups provides a different thickening mechanism from salt thickening or CDEA systems.

CondiSoft BTMS

Behentrimonium Methosulfate 25%

Emulsifying ConditionerCationic EmulsifierPremium Hair Care

"The cationic emulsifier that makes conditioners possible — BTMS is the structural and conditioning foundation of every premium rinse-out conditioner."

CondiSoft BTMS is Behentrimonium Methosulfate (BTMS) at 25% in cetearyl alcohol — the definitive cationic emulsifier for rinse-out conditioners, deep conditioning masks and leave-in treatments. BTMS provides a positively charged emulsifier matrix that creates stable oil-in-water emulsions with inherent conditioning deposition — unlike non-ionic emulsifiers that form neutral films, BTMS deposits a cationic conditioning layer on hair fibres during the rinse-out process.

The combination of emulsification, conditioning and substantivity in a single raw material enables the formulation of conditioning products with a significantly simpler ingredient list than traditional petrolatum/lanolin-based formulations. The cetearyl alcohol co-component provides additional structure and a rich, luxurious product texture that consumers associate with premium conditioning.

CondiSoft BTMS is the primary active in the majority of DIY and professional natural conditioner formulations globally and is the standard starting material for natural hair care product development — suitable for all hair types with particular efficacy for high-porosity, chemically treated and coily/kinky hair textures.

CondiVeil 949

Amodimethicone, Cetrimonium Chloride & Trideceth-12

Silicone ConditionerTargeted DepositionFrizz Control

"Reactive silicone with built-in deposition technology — the premium finishing active for high-shine, frizz-free hair care."

CondiVeil 949 is a pre-formulated conditioning emulsion combining Amodimethicone, Cetrimonium Chloride and Trideceth-12 — a patented delivery system that provides highly substantive silicone deposition on damaged hair while remaining compatible with anionic surfactant cleansing bases. Standard silicone fluids (dimethicone) deposit non-selectively on all areas of the hair shaft; CondiVeil 949's amodimethicone is an amino-functional silicone that deposits preferentially on the sites of greatest damage — the areas with the highest density of negative charge on chemically treated or mechanically damaged hair.

This targeted deposition mechanism means CondiVeil 949 provides the most conditioning benefit exactly where the hair needs it most, without build-up on undamaged sections. The cetrimonium chloride component enables compatibility with anionic surfactant systems — allowing direct incorporation into 2-in-1 shampoos and conditioning cleansers where conventional silicone emulsions would precipitate.
