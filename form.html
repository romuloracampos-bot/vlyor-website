The Emulsifier Argument

Everything in cosmetics
is, at its core, an
emulsification problem.

A moisturiser is oil-in-water. A sunscreen stick is water-in-oil. A serum is an aqueous gel. Every product category is defined by how its phases are held together — and the answer is emulsifier selection. Choose the wrong emulsifier and the formula separates, discolours or fails stability within weeks. Choose the right one and you have a product that survives the LATAM supply chain — 3–6 months from manufacturing to consumer through temperature extremes and humidity variation.

VLYOR FORM addresses this problem from every angle. FormEssence fatty alcohol and castor oil ethoxylates (HLB 6–19) cover the O/W emulsifier and solubiliser spectrum. SorbEssence sorbitan esters (HLB 1.8–8.6) provide the W/O emulsifier range. FormBase ceteareth and steareth emulsifiers deliver the proven classical O/W bases. RheoForm carbomers and biopolymers build the gel and viscosity architecture. EsterSilk, TricaPure and SqualEssence complete formulas with the emollient character that defines final sensory profile.

"An emulsifier is not a commodity — it is a structural decision. The same oil phase, the same active and the same water phase will produce completely different stability, sensory and performance profiles depending solely on which emulsifier you choose."

FormEssence O

Nonionic EmulsifierHLB 6–199 GradesO/W + Solubiliser

"Nine fatty alcohol ethoxylate grades covering HLB 6 to 19 — the most versatile nonionic emulsifier series in the FORM portfolio, covering O/W emulsification and solubilisation across the full HLB range."

FormEssence O Series are fatty alcohol ethoxylates (FAE) produced by ethoxylation of C12–C18 linear fatty alcohols with 3 to 50 EO units. Each additional EO unit adds ~0.5 HLB, enabling precise emulsifier selection for any oil phase or solubilisation requirement.

Low-EO grades (O-3, O-5) — W/O co-emulsifiers. Mid-EO (O-8 to O-10, HLB 12–14) — primary O/W emulsifiers for skin care creams, optimal for mineral oils, esters and silicones. High-EO (O-20 to O-50) — solubilisers for fragrance, essential oils and fat-soluble vitamins in clear aqueous systems.

FormBase SA

Stearic Acid · CAS 57-11-4 · C18 Saturated Fatty Acid

Fatty Acid EmulsifierSelf-EmulsifyingConsistency FactorCOSMOS-eligible

"Stearic Acid — the foundational anionic emulsifier for self-emulsifying cream systems. Neutralised with alkali (TEA, NaOH) it forms in-situ soap emulsifiers, the basis of vanishing creams and traditional LATAM mass-market skin care."

FormBase SA is cosmetic-grade Stearic Acid — saturated C18 fatty acid. At cosmetic use levels (5–15%), its emulsification function is activated by partial in-situ neutralisation with alkali: TEA or NaOH converts approximately 20% of the stearic acid to stearate soap emulsifier while the remaining 80% provides consistency. This soap emulsifies the oil phase, creating the characteristic opaque, waxy cream texture of traditional skin care.

The resulting soap-emulsified cream has distinctive properties: slightly alkaline pH (6.5–7.5), waxy-creamy opacity, excellent skin feel, and self-emulsifying stability that improves over 24–48 hours as the soap equilibrates. This system underpins cold creams, vanishing creams and traditional skin care formulations that remain commercially important in the LATAM mass market — particularly Brazil, Colombia and Peru.

When extracted from olive squalene and hydrogenated to squalane, the compound loses the six double bonds of squalene (eliminating oxidative instability) while retaining the complete biological affinity for human skin. This skin-identical structure is the basis of squalane's commercially unmatched emollient character: it integrates seamlessly into the stratum corneum lipid matrix, providing barrier reinforcement and moisture retention that synthetic esters and silicones cannot achieve.

SqualEssence provides three commercially unique properties: a silky, lightweight skin feel despite providing genuine moisturisation (no greasiness, no residue); exceptional active stabilisation (squalane protects oxidation-sensitive actives including retinol, Vitamin C derivatives and polyunsaturated fatty acids through its free radical scavenging properties); and outstanding skin compatibility (non-comedogenic, appropriate for sensitive, acne-prone and mature skin alike). It is the preferred emollient for prestige serums, face oils and anti-ageing formulations globally.

In practice

HLB 4.7
Emulsifier selection determines the stability outcome

An emulsifier selected at the wrong HLB for the oil phase doesn't produce an unstable emulsion immediately — it produces one that fails at month 3 of accelerated stability testing, after the formula has already been through regulatory review and consumer testing. SorbEssence S-60 at HLB 4.7 paired correctly with Polysorbate 60 prevents that failure. The cost of the correct emulsifier is negligible. The cost of the wrong one is the entire development cycle.

8 grades
AquaForm PEG: one INCI, eight formulation solutions

PEG 200 solubilises fragrance in a clear toner. PEG 6000 provides waxy consistency in an anhydrous stick. Eight grades from the same supplier, same INCI, same documentation package — with a single supplier relationship, single purchase order process and single technical contact for the entire range. That supply simplification has an operational value that doesn't appear on the ingredient spec sheet.

0.5%
Carbomer at the right grade makes or breaks a gel serum

RheoForm 980 at 0.5% produces a water-clear gel with zero measurable turbidity — the transparency standard for premium serums sold in glass packaging where clarity is a visible quality signal. RheoForm 940 at the same concentration produces a gel that is slightly hazy. That optical difference is not visible on the spec sheet. It is visible to every consumer who picks up the product.
