Product Overview

ChelaGuard 2 is Disodium EDTA at ≥99.0% purity — ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid disodium salt, the most widely used chelating agent in personal care globally. Its four carboxylate and two amine donor atoms form stable, water-soluble complexes with divalent and trivalent metal cations — Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Fe²⁺, Fe³⁺, Cu²⁺, Mn²⁺ — present in trace concentrations throughout the manufacturing environment: in process water, raw material impurities and packaging extractables.

Left unchelated, these metal ions catalyse a cascade of degradation reactions: oxidative degradation of Vitamin C, retinoids, tocopherols and fragrances via Fenton-type radical generation; rancidity of lipid phases via metal-catalysed autoxidation; and systematic undermining of preservative efficacy through competitive binding to preservative active sites. ChelaGuard 2 at 0.05–0.5% comprehensively blocks all three degradation pathways simultaneously.

Mechanism of Action — Chelation Chemistry

EDTA is a hexadentate ligand that forms thermodynamically stable octahedral complexes with most divalent and trivalent metal cations. The stability constants (log K) of key EDTA-metal complexes range from 14.3 (Ca²⁺) to 25.1 (Fe³⁺), ensuring effectively quantitative metal sequestration at pH 4–9 and at cosmetic use concentrations. The chelated metal is rendered catalytically inactive — it cannot participate in Fenton reactions, cannot bridge bacterial outer membrane lipopolysaccharide chains and cannot catalyse lipid peroxidation. The EDTA-metal complex is water-soluble and stable throughout the product's shelf life. Preservative booster mechanism: gram-negative bacteria (particularly Pseudomonas) maintain outer membrane integrity through Mg²⁺/Ca²⁺ bridges between lipopolysaccharide chains. EDTA sequesters these structural cations, disrupting the outer membrane and rendering the bacteria dramatically more susceptible to the primary preservative at equivalent concentration.

Product Overview

ChelaGuard 4 is Tetrasodium EDTA at ≥99.0% purity — the fully neutralised tetrasodium salt of ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid. While chemically identical to Disodium EDTA in its chelating function, the fully neutralised form creates a strongly alkaline aqueous solution (pH 11–12 at 1%) that makes it the appropriate choice for high-pH formulation systems where ChelaGuard 2 would lower pH and where the alkaline environment optimises tetrasodium EDTA's chelation efficiency.

Preferred in oxidative hair colourant systems (hydrogen peroxide-based, pH 9–11), hair bleaching formulas (pH 9.5–12), alkaline hair relaxers (pH 12–14, thioglycolate or sodium hydroxide-based) and high-pH cleansers and industrial personal care where the manufacturing and in-use pH consistently exceeds 7 and disodium EDTA's chelation efficiency and solubility characteristics are suboptimal.

Differentiation from ChelaGuard 2 (Disodium EDTA)

Both disodium and tetrasodium EDTA chelate metal ions through the same hexadentate complexation mechanism. The key practical difference is the degree of neutralisation and resulting solution pH. Disodium EDTA (two of the four carboxylic acid groups neutralised) produces pH 4–5 in solution — ideal for acidic to neutral cosmetic systems. Tetrasodium EDTA (all four groups neutralised) produces pH 11–12 — providing chelation without additional acidification in alkaline systems. In alkaline manufacturing, adding ChelaGuard 2 would require pH correction; ChelaGuard 4 is added without affecting the system pH balance. Sequential use strategy: use ChelaGuard 4 during alkaline processing stages (colour mixing, bleach preparation) and ChelaGuard 2 in the final acidified finished product where applicable.

Product Overview

SoluCast 40 is PEG-40 Hydrogenated Castor Oil (HCO-40) — produced by ethoxylation of hydrogenated castor oil (predominantly 12-hydroxystearic acid triglyceride) with 40 moles of ethylene oxide. The resulting non-ionic polyethoxylated ester carries a high HLB value of approximately 13–14, enabling effective solubilisation of a wide range of oil-soluble fragrance compounds, essential oils, fat-soluble vitamins and cosmetic actives into transparent aqueous formulations while maintaining optical clarity at higher oil loads than polysorbate 20 alone.

The practical differentiation from polysorbates arises from the castor oil ester core: while polysorbates are built on sorbitan esters with no skin-functional contribution, HCO-40's 12-hydroxystearic acid polyhydroxy fatty acid structure provides a mild emollient and conditioning character to the finished product. This makes SoluCast 40 preferred in leave-on transparent formulations — toners, clear serums, face mists — where the solubiliser is part of the final formula and a positive skin feel is commercially important.

Solubilisation Mechanism

HCO-40 solubilises oil-soluble compounds through micellar encapsulation. Above its critical micellar concentration (typically 0.01–0.05%), HCO-40 molecules self-assemble into spherical micelles with a hydrophobic core formed by the hydrogenated castor oil acyl chains. Oil-soluble ingredients partition into this hydrophobic core, increasing their apparent aqueous solubility by several orders of magnitude. The outer polyethylene oxide chains (40 EO units) provide strong hydration and steric stabilisation, maintaining micellar stability across a wide pH and temperature range. Ratio guidance: start at 2:1 to 4:1 (solubiliser:oil by weight) and optimise for each fragrance or oil based on polarity. Complex fragrance blends with high-polarity components may require reduced ratios; purely hydrocarbon-type essential oils may require 4:1 or higher.

Product Overview

PreserFlex PE is Phenoxyethanol at ≥99.5% purity — 2-phenoxyethanol, the single most commercially used cosmetic preservative globally by formulation frequency. Its amphiphilic structure — an aromatic phenyl ether with a polar ethanol tail — enables it to partition into microbial cell membranes, disrupting membrane fluidity and permeability and ultimately disabling the proton motive force required for bacterial ATP synthesis. This membrane-disruption mechanism provides bacteriostatic and bactericidal activity against a broad range of bacteria and yeasts at 0.5–1.0%.

Phenoxyethanol's commercial dominance arises from properties that no single alternative fully replicates: broad-spectrum coverage across gram-positive, gram-negative bacteria and yeasts; stability across pH 3–10; compatibility with all formulation matrices; and regulatory approval at 1% in every major cosmetic market globally. It can be incorporated into aqueous or oil phases and is stable throughout the manufacturing process including heating to 80°C.

Mechanism of Action — Membrane Disruption + EDTA Synergy

Phenoxyethanol partitions into microbial phospholipid bilayers based on its log P (~1.16) — enough lipophilicity for membrane association, enough hydrophilicity for aqueous delivery. Once inserted, it disorders membrane phospholipid packing, increases membrane permeability to protons and small ions, and disrupts the proton-motive force (PMF) driving ATP synthesis. The gram-negative limitation: gram-negative bacteria possess an outer membrane (OM) as a permeability barrier exterior to the cytoplasmic membrane. The OM's lipopolysaccharide layer is stabilised by Mg²⁺/Ca²⁺ bridges — if these are intact, PE cannot reach the cytoplasmic membrane at practical concentrations. ChelaGuard 2 (EDTA) resolves this: EDTA chelates the OM cations, destabilising the LPS layer and allowing PE to access and disrupt the cytoplasmic membrane. PE without EDTA fails ISO 11930 gram-negative criteria in approximately 40–60% of formulation types; PE + EDTA achieves compliant results in >95% of applications.

Product Overview

PreserFlex EHG is a precision blend of Phenoxyethanol and Ethylhexylglycerin in a 9:1 ratio — the most commercially specified modern cosmetic preservation system globally, designed to address phenoxyethanol's primary performance limitation: moderate efficacy against gram-positive bacteria (Staphylococcus aureus) and yeasts (Candida albicans) at practical use concentrations.

Ethylhexylglycerin (2-ethylhexyl glycerine ether) is a membrane-active sensitiser: its amphiphilic glycerol ether structure inserts into microbial cell membranes, reducing interfacial surface tension and disrupting membrane phospholipid organisation. This membrane destabilisation does not itself kill microorganisms at cosmetic use concentrations, but it dramatically lowers the minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) of phenoxyethanol for gram-positive organisms — in effect converting resistant strains into sensitive ones. The resulting combination achieves ISO 11930 A/B compliance across all five challenge organisms at 0.7–0.9% total blend, versus 0.9–1.0% phenoxyethanol alone and without the gram-positive coverage assurance.

Dual Mechanism — Membrane Disruption + Sensitisation

Phenoxyethanol component (90%): disrupts cytoplasmic membrane integrity through phospholipid disordering, disabling proton motive force and ATP synthesis. Active against gram-negative, gram-positive bacteria and yeasts. Ethylhexylglycerin component (10%): reduces membrane interfacial tension through glycerol ether intercalation into phospholipid headgroup regions. Does not independently kill organisms at cosmetic concentrations, but lowers the PE concentration required for MIC against gram-positive bacteria and Candida from approximately 0.8–1.0% to 0.6–0.8% — enabling full broad-spectrum coverage at a lower total preservative load. Combined with ChelaGuard 2, which completes gram-negative (Pseudomonas) coverage, the three-component system (PE/EHG/EDTA) is the most widely used broad-spectrum preservation approach in premium cosmetics globally, achieving reliable ISO 11930 Category A/B compliance across all five challenge organisms in the vast majority of formulation types.

Product Overview

PreserFlex MP is Methylparaben at ≥99.5% purity — methyl 4-hydroxybenzoate, a para-hydroxybenzoate ester with an 80-year commercial safety and efficacy record, the most extensively studied preservative in cosmetic science. Methylparaben operates through inhibition of essential microbial enzymes (particularly dehydrogenases) and disruption of cell membrane transport function, with particular effectiveness against gram-positive bacteria (Staphylococcus aureus, Bacillus subtilis) and fungi/yeasts (Candida albicans, Aspergillus brasiliensis) — the organism classes most frequently implicated in cosmetic product spoilage.

The scientific and regulatory consensus on methylparaben safety is clear: EU Annex V listed at 0.4% (solo) or 0.8% (mixed parabens), WHO-evaluated and approved, and supported by the scientific opinion of the EU Scientific Committee on Consumer Safety (SCCS) confirming safety at current use levels. In LATAM markets — where the clean beauty movement has penetrated less deeply than in Western Europe — methylparaben remains widely commercially accepted and enables cost-effective preservation with proven challenge-test performance.

Mechanism of Action — Enzymatic Inhibition + Membrane Disruption

Methylparaben inhibits microbial membrane transport enzymes including Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase and membrane-associated dehydrogenases, disrupting cellular energy metabolism and active transport of nutrients across the membrane. The phenolic hydroxyl group is essential for activity — it is believed to interact with key enzymatic thiol and amino groups through hydrogen bonding and hydrophobic interactions. Methylparaben is effective predominantly at acidic to neutral pH (4–8) — above pH 8, the phenolate form predominates and activity decreases. This explains the recommendation to use ChelaGuard 2 (or ChelaGuard 4) together with methylparaben in all applications: the gram-negative bacteria (Pseudomonas, E. coli) are poorly controlled by methylparaben alone but respond strongly to PE or EHG combined with EDTA-mediated membrane sensitisation.

Product Overview

PreserFlex DMDM is DMDM Hydantoin — 1,3-bis(hydroxymethyl)-5,5-dimethylhydantoin, a formaldehyde-releasing preservative (FRP) that generates free formaldehyde in aqueous systems through hydrolysis of its N-hydroxymethyl bonds. The released formaldehyde cross-links microbial proteins (by reacting with amino groups in proteins and nucleic acid bases), providing continuous low-level antimicrobial protection throughout product shelf life through sustained slow-release rather than a one-time dose.

PreserFlex DMDM's primary commercial application is rinse-off hair care — shampoos, conditioners, scalp treatments and hair masks — where its outstanding compatibility with anionic and amphoteric surfactant systems, stable efficacy at pH 5–8 and highly competitive cost-performance ratio make it the preferred preservative for mass-market hair care production. Its water-solubility enables simple aqueous-phase incorporation without pre-dissolution steps.

Mechanism of Action — Controlled Formaldehyde Release

DMDM Hydantoin releases formaldehyde through slow hydrolysis of N-CH₂OH bonds in aqueous solution. The equilibrium formaldehyde concentration in the final product is typically 30–100 ppm at normal use levels — well below the EU 100 ppm label declaration threshold at concentrations ≤0.6% (as HCHO). The released HCHO reacts with primary amines, thiol groups and guanine bases in microbial proteins and DNA, cross-linking cellular macromolecules and preventing microbial replication. Continuous replenishment: as free formaldehyde is consumed by microbial reaction, the equilibrium shifts to release additional formaldehyde from the DMDM reservoir, providing sustained protection throughout shelf life. This sustained-release mechanism differentiates DMDM from direct formaldehyde addition. Positioning note: for products where "formaldehyde-free" is a consumer-facing claim or a brand commitment, specify PreserFlex PE or PreserFlex EHG instead.

Product Overview

ClimbaFlex is Climbazole at ≥99.0% purity — 1-(4-chlorophenoxy)-1-imidazol-1-yl-3,3-dimethylbutan-2-one, an imidazole-class antifungal cosmetic active with high specific efficacy against Malassezia furfur, M. globosa, M. restricta and related lipophilic Malassezia species. These organisms are the primary — and in the vast majority of cases, the sole — causative agents of seborrhoeic dermatitis and dandruff, affecting approximately 50% of the adult population in some form and up to 80% in high-humidity tropical climates.

ClimbaFlex is the reference cosmetic anti-dandruff active in the European and LATAM professional market, used at 0.5% in the majority of premium anti-dandruff shampoos and scalp treatment formulas. Its oil solubility enables critical sebum-mediated scalp deposition: upon rinse-off application, climbazole partitions preferentially into the sebum layer on the scalp surface — the precise lipid-rich environment where Malassezia preferentially colonises and replicates — providing residual antifungal activity between washes that water-soluble alternatives (zinc pyrithione, selenium sulfide, piroctone olamine) cannot match in leave-on efficacy.

Mechanism of Action — Ergosterol Biosynthesis Inhibition

Climbazole inhibits the fungal cytochrome P450 enzyme lanosterol 14α-demethylase (CYP51) — the enzyme responsible for converting lanosterol to ergosterol in the fungal cell membrane biosynthesis pathway. Ergosterol is the primary sterol component of fungal cell membranes (equivalent to cholesterol in mammalian membranes); its depletion disrupts membrane fluidity, permeability and integrity, ultimately preventing fungal cell replication. The imidazole nitrogen of climbazole coordinates with the haem iron of the fungal CYP51 enzyme active site, blocking substrate access with high affinity (Ki in the nanomolar range for Malassezia CYP51). Selectivity: the fungal CYP51 has substantially different substrate binding characteristics from mammalian sterol metabolism enzymes, explaining climbazole's excellent safety profile at scalp application concentrations. Minimum fungicidal concentration (MFC) against Malassezia: typically 0.001–0.01% — meaning the 0.5% standard formulation dose provides a 50–500-fold safety margin relative to the minimum effective antifungal concentration, ensuring efficacy even accounting for dilution effects in rinse-off application.

Product Overview

AntipersFlex ACH is Aluminum Chlorohydrate at ≥20% Al₂O₃ equivalent — a polymeric aluminium hydroxychloride complex that is the most widely used antiperspirant active globally by formulation volume. ACH is a true antiperspirant: it reduces wetness by forming physical gel plugs within eccrine sweat gland ducts, mechanically restricting secretion of sweat to the skin surface — a fundamentally different mode of action from deodorant actives (which address odour without reducing perspiration volume).

The mechanism requires aluminium ion interaction with eccrine sweat secretion at the duct opening: Al³⁺ ions react with electrolytes in the sweat to form insoluble aluminium hydroxide hydrogels that physically occlude the ductal lumen. This process is reversible and temporary — the gel plugs dissolve over time (typically 24–48 hours without reapplication) as sweat production and pH changes dissolve the aluminium hydroxide structure. The 48-hour claim standard for ACH-based products reflects typical plug durability under moderate sweat load conditions.

Physical Occlusion Mechanism + Grade Selection

ACH is supplied in three grades matched to formulation format. Anhydrous powder grade: used in pressed stick, compressed powder and aerosol-format antiperspirants where water-free processing is required and the active is dispersed in anhydrous vehicles (cyclomethicones, waxes, esters). The anhydrous form provides higher active loading per unit weight and is essential for achieving antiperspirant claims in dry formulation formats. 20% aqueous solution grade: standard for roll-on antiperspirants where the active is delivered in aqueous vehicle. Simple addition to the water phase; moderate viscosity solution with pH 4.0–4.5. 50% solution grade: high-concentration grade for applications requiring maximum active loading in aqueous systems. Requires controlled pH management during formulation due to the higher acidity of the concentrated solution.

Product Overview

AntipersFlex AZG is Aluminum Zirconium Tetrachlorohydrex Glycine (ZAG complex) — a multi-metal coordination compound in which aluminium and zirconium are co-complexed with the amino acid glycine as a polymeric bridging ligand. The resulting ZAG polymer forms a materially more robust, less hydrophilic and denser gel plug within the eccrine sweat duct versus aluminium chlorohydrate alone, providing stronger initial occlusion and significantly longer-lasting antiperspirant efficacy between applications.

ZAG is the active ingredient in every major clinical-strength, 72-hour, sport-performance and enhanced-protection antiperspirant marketed globally by leading personal care brands. In the LATAM premium antiperspirant segment — driven by high ambient temperatures (requiring genuinely superior sweat control), growing sport and active lifestyle consumer positioning, and increasing premiumisation of personal care — ZAG-based products command a significant price premium and represent the fastest-growing segment of the antiperspirant category. AntipersFlex AZG is supplied exclusively in anhydrous powder form for compatibility with dry-manufacturing processes used in stick, aerosol and compressed format production.

Enhanced Mechanism — ZAG vs. ACH Duct Occlusion

The performance advantage of ZAG arises from the mixed Al/Zr metal complex with glycine bridging. Zirconium forms more stable, less hydrolysis-reversible hydroxide gels at physiological pH than aluminium alone — the resulting aluminium-zirconium hydroxide complex has a higher cross-link density and lower water solubility than the aluminium hydroxide formed from ACH. The glycine amino acid acts as a polymeric bridging ligand between Al and Zr metal centres, creating a network structure that forms a mechanically stiffer, more occlusive plug within the eccrine duct. Clinical comparison data (internal formulation reference): ZAG at equivalent Al metal dosing provides 15–30% greater sweat reduction at 24 hours versus ACH and maintains statistically significant superiority at 48 and 72 hours post-application. This clinical differentiation — supported by published comparative efficacy data for commercial ZAG products — directly justifies the premium pricing of clinical-strength antiperspirant products in LATAM markets and globally.
