The Hydration Argument

Moisture retention is
not a claim. It is
a formulation decision.

Every cosmetic formula exists in a constant battle with water loss. Emulsions lose water to evaporation. Gels lose water to the environment. Hair loses water under thermal stress. The ingredients that determine whether a formula wins or loses this battle are humectants and film formers — the invisible hydration infrastructure of personal care.

VLYOR HYDRA provides the complete humectant and film-former toolkit for LATAM formulation: the AquaForm PEG series spanning every molecular weight from mobile liquid to waxy solid; the HydraBase glycol family covering the four most commercially important humectant glycols; and the AquaFilm PVP series delivering transparent, shiny, adjustable-hold film-forming performance for hair care across the full styling spectrum.

"A humectant is the formula's promise to the skin: I will draw moisture to you and hold it there. Every other moisturisation mechanism depends on this first, foundational commitment."

VLYOR Science — Hydration Architecture Technical Position

In the LATAM climate — where humidity varies from the tropical moisture of São Paulo and Bogotá to the arid conditions of Mexico City and Santiago — humectant selection is not neutral. The wrong humectant at the wrong concentration produces sticky, heavy skin feel in humid conditions, or insufficient moisturisation in dry conditions. VLYOR HYDRA provides the molecular toolkit to engineer moisture behaviour precisely for the target climate.

AquaForm PEG

Polyethylene Glycol (PEG) · CAS 25322-68-3 · Grades: PEG 200 · 400 · 600 · 1000 · 1500 · 4000 · 6000 · 8000

PEG Humectant8 GradesPEG 200–8000Liquid to Solid

"Eight grades of Polyethylene Glycol spanning molecular weight 200 to 8000 — from mobile clear liquids for humectancy and solubilisation through waxy solid flakes for consistency modification, ointment bases and sustained moisture release."

AquaForm PEG Series is Polyethylene Glycol (PEG) — a family of linear polyether polymers produced by polymerisation of ethylene oxide with repeating —CH⊂2;—CH⊂2;—O— units. The molecular weight and consequent physical form change systematically with chain length: PEG 200, 400, 600 are clear, colourless liquids with increasing viscosity; PEG 1000–1500 are pastes or soft solids; PEG 4000, 6000 and 8000 are white waxy flakes with melting points of approximately 55–65°C. This molecular weight spectrum produces fundamentally different formulation functions from a single INCI chemistry.

Liquid grades (PEG 200–600) act as humectants, solvents and solubilisers — attracting moisture from the environment through multiple hydroxyl groups, dissolving polar actives that are insoluble in water or glycols, and reducing the stickiness of high-glycerin formulations when blended in. PEG 400 is the most commercially used grade globally, providing balanced humectancy with low viscosity and broad compatibility. Solid grades (PEG 4000–8000) function as consistency factors, ointment bases, tablet binders and water-washable bases — providing waxy structure in anhydrous formulations, improving skin feel in cold cream systems and acting as water-soluble alternatives to traditional waxes. All grades meet USP/NF pharmacopoeial standards as supplied.

HydraBase Glyc

Glycerin (Glycerol) · CAS 56-81-5 · USP / BP / EP · Vegetable Origin

HumectantNatural Origin25% water bindingUSP / BP / EP

"Glycerin — the most widely used humectant in cosmetics globally. USP / BP / EP pharmacopoeial quality from vegetable origin, providing the highest water-binding capacity of all glycols at 25% of its weight at 50% relative humidity."

HydraBase Glyc is Glycerin (Glycerol) — the triol humectant with three hydroxyl groups per molecule, CAS 56-81-5. Glycerin is the most commercially important humectant in personal care, present in the majority of moisturisers, creams, lotions, shampoos and body washes globally. Its three —OH groups provide the highest water-binding capacity of any standard cosmetic glycol: at 50% relative humidity, glycerin absorbs approximately 25% of its own weight in water from the environment — drawing moisture continuously from the ambient air into the formulation and skin surface.

HydraBase Glyc is supplied to USP / BP / EP pharmacopoeial quality from vegetable origin (primarily a by-product of biodiesel / fatty acid production from plant oils), making it appropriate for natural-positioned, organic and COSMOS-eligible formulations. The principal formulation challenge of glycerin is its pronounced stickiness at high concentrations (>5–8% in leave-on products) — which is managed by blending with PEG 400 (AquaForm), propylene glycol (HydraBase PG) or other humectants that reduce perceived stickiness without reducing water binding. In rinse-off products (shampoos, conditioners, body washes), glycerin concentrations of 1–3% provide effective moisturisation without stickiness issues.

HydraBase PG

Propylene Glycol (1,2-Propanediol) · CAS 57-55-6 · USP Grade

Humectant / Solvent20% water bindingUSP GradeLow Viscosity

"Propylene Glycol — the workhorse multifunctional humectant. USP quality, low viscosity, outstanding solubilisation of fragrances and actives, non-sticky feel and exceptional compatibility across the full cosmetic formulation range."

HydraBase PG is Propylene Glycol (1,2-Propanediol) — the diol humectant with two hydroxyl groups per molecule, CAS 57-55-6. It is the second most widely used humectant in cosmetics after glycerin, providing water-binding at 50% RH of approximately 20% of its weight — slightly below glycerin but without glycerin's stickiness at comparable concentrations. Its low molecular weight (76 g/mol), low viscosity (~56 mPa·s at 25°C) and outstanding compatibility with a broad range of cosmetic ingredients make it the most versatile multifunctional humectant available.

Beyond humectancy, HydraBase PG provides three additional formulation functions simultaneously: it acts as a solubiliser for fragrance materials and lipophilic actives that are insoluble in water; as an antimicrobial agent at concentrations >15%, improving preservation efficacy of primary preservatives; and as a penetration enhancer, improving the transdermal delivery of solubilised actives at concentrations of 5–15%. This combination of functions from a single, cost-effective ingredient explains its position as the cosmetic industry's most widely used multifunctional active.

HydraBase DPG

Dipropylene Glycol (DPG) · CAS 25265-71-8 · Fragrance Grade

Humectant / Fragrance CarrierPremium FeelFragrance GradeLow Odour

"Dipropylene Glycol — the premium humectant and fragrance carrier. Higher molecular weight than Propylene Glycol produces a lighter, drier skin feel with less tackiness and superior fragrance-carrying capacity. Standard carrier for perfume concentrates in personal care."

HydraBase DPG is Dipropylene Glycol (DPG) — a mixture of three isomers of dipropylene glycol produced as a by-product of propylene glycol manufacture, CAS 25265-71-8. Its higher molecular weight versus PG (134 g/mol vs. 76 g/mol) produces a liquid with slightly higher viscosity (~110 mPa·s at 25°C) and a markedly lighter, less tacky skin feel than propylene glycol at equivalent concentrations. This improved aesthetic profile makes DPG the preferred humectant for premium skin care formulations and products where skin feel is a primary commercial differentiator.

DPG is also the standard carrier solvent for fragrance concentrates in personal care: its low odour, excellent solubilisation of essential oils and synthetic fragrance materials, and compatibility with both water-soluble and oil-soluble perfume components make it the industry-standard fragrance carrier in fine fragrance, body mist and eau de toilette formulations. In personal care products more broadly, it is used as a dual-function ingredient — simultaneously providing humectancy and serving as the fragrance delivery vehicle — reducing total ingredient count while delivering both functions.

HydraBase PDO

1,3-Propanediol (Propanediol) · CAS 504-63-2 · Bio-Based Origin Available

HumectantBio-Based Origin~18% water bindingPG Alternative

"1,3-Propanediol — the bio-based Propylene Glycol alternative with superior skin feel and clean-label positioning. Same molecular formula as PG but different structure: lighter, less tacky and naturally derivable from corn sugar fermentation."

HydraBase PDO is 1,3-Propanediol (Propanediol) — a diol humectant isomeric to propylene glycol, CAS 504-63-2. While it shares the molecular formula C⊂3;H⊂8;O⊂2; with propylene glycol (1,2-Propanediol), the hydroxyl groups are positioned at carbons 1 and 3 rather than 1 and 2, producing a molecule with different physical-chemical properties: lower viscosity, lighter skin feel, reduced tackiness and superior skin moisturisation compared to PG at equivalent loading.

The defining commercial differentiator of PDO is its bio-based origin: 1,3-Propanediol can be produced by fermentation of corn sugar (glucose), providing a plant-derived equivalent to the traditionally petroleum-derived propylene glycol. This bio-origin makes HydraBase PDO the correct specification for natural-positioned, COSMOS-formulated and clean-label products where petroleum-derived propylene glycol is excluded from the ingredient brief. At equivalent concentrations, PDO provides comparable water-binding to PG with a meaningfully improved sensory profile — lighter, drier and less tacky — enabling higher humectant loading without consumer perception of stickiness.

AquaFilm K30

Polyvinylpyrrolidone K30 · CAS 9003-39-8 · MW ~40,000–58,000 · Film Former

Film FormerPVP K30Medium MWHair Styling

"PVP K30 — the medium molecular weight polyvinylpyrrolidone for spray and liquid styling applications. Produces stiff, transparent, shiny films from clear solution at 3–6% loading. The reference film former for pump sprays, styling lotions and setting preparations."

AquaFilm K30 is Polyvinylpyrrolidone at K-value 30 (PVP K30) — a nonionic, water-soluble homopolymer of N-vinylpyrrolidone with molecular weight approximately 40,000–58,000 g/mol. The K-value is a measure of relative viscosity in dilute solution — K30 designating the medium molecular weight grade. PVP is the reference nonionic hair styling polymer globally, having been the first synthetic polymer applied as a hair fixative in the 1950s as a replacement for shellac. It forms stiff, transparent, highly glossy films on hair fibres when the water carrier evaporates after product application.

The medium molecular weight of K30 produces the ideal viscosity-performance balance for spray applications: at 3–6% in water or water-alcohol vehicle, K30 solutions remain sufficiently low viscosity to pass through pump sprays, aerosol valves and fine mist nozzles without clogging, while delivering adequate film-forming molecular weight for hair hold performance. PVP K30 is fully soluble in water and most polar organic solvents including ethanol — compatible with alcohol-containing hair spray formulations — and compatible with a broad range of cosmetic co-polymers, polyquaternium conditioning polymers and fixative boosters. Films formed at low relative humidity are crisp and stiff; at high humidity they absorb atmospheric moisture and soften, which can be managed through blending with less hygroscopic co-formers.

AquaFilm K90

Polyvinylpyrrolidone K90 · CAS 9003-39-8 · MW ~900,000–1,500,000 · High-Hold Film Former

Film FormerPVP K90High MWMaximum Hold

"PVP K90 — the high molecular weight polyvinylpyrrolidone for maximum hold and viscosity-critical styling applications. Higher MW produces stronger, more durable hair films with significantly greater hold force than K30 at equivalent concentration."

AquaFilm K90 is Polyvinylpyrrolidone at K-value 90 (PVP K90) — the high molecular weight grade of PVP with molecular weight approximately 900,000–1,500,000 g/mol. The dramatically higher molecular weight versus K30 (~40,000) produces fundamentally different film properties: significantly stronger, more durable films with higher hold force per unit mass of polymer, better adhesion to hair fibres and superior resistance to humidity-induced film softening. At equivalent polymer concentration, K90 delivers substantially greater styling hold than K30.

The primary formulation trade-off of K90 is viscosity: at 3–6% (the K30 use range), K90 produces excessively viscous solutions incompatible with spray applications. K90 is therefore used at lower concentrations (1–3%) where it provides equivalent or superior hold to K30 at 3–6%, or it is blended with K30 to achieve an intermediate MW profile with controlled solution viscosity. Pure K90 applications are primarily gel-format styling products (hair gel, wet-look gel, pomade) where high viscosity is a desired product attribute rather than a processing limitation.
