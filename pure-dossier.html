Product Overview

GlucoPure C810 Series is Caprylyl/Capryl Glucoside — the C8/C10 Alkyl Polyglucoside (APG) produced by acid-catalysed Fischer glycosidation of glucose with a C8/C10 fatty alcohol blend derived from renewable coconut and palm kernel fractions. APGs represent the state of the art in biosourced surfactant chemistry: fully derived from renewable carbohydrate and fatty alcohol feedstocks, readily biodegradable per OECD 301B standard, and carrying COSMOS-organic and COSMOS-natural certification eligibility for natural cosmetic formulation.

The short C8/C10 chain length positions GlucoPure C810 as the mildest APG in the PURE portfolio. In-vitro keratinocyte and in-vivo HRIPT studies confirm near-zero transepidermal water loss alteration at use concentrations, establishing it as the reference non-ionic surfactant for products targeting baby, sensitive skin and intimate hygiene categories where clinical mildness certification is required. Available in multiple grades — HSO810, HS215 and HS225N — with varying active content, APE specification and cloud point to match different formulation requirements.

Mechanism of Action

APG surfactants function through classical micelle formation. The glucoside headgroup is markedly larger and more hydrophilic than conventional non-ionic headgroups (polyethoxylates), generating micelles with larger hydrodynamic radii and lower free monomer concentration at equivalent CMC. This structural feature directly explains the reduced skin penetration and irritation profile of APGs relative to conventional anionic and non-ionic surfactants: it is the monomeric surfactant — not the micellar form — that penetrates the stratum corneum and elicits inflammatory response. Caprylyl/Capryl Glucoside combines this structural advantage with the minimum viable alkyl chain length, producing the lowest irritation index within the glucoside family.

Product Overview

GlucoPure CO is Coco Glucoside — produced from the condensation of coconut fatty alcohol (C12–C14 distribution) with glucose. The broad chain-length distribution relative to single-chain APGs delivers a balanced foam-cleansing-mildness profile across a wide formulation pH range, making it the most versatile primary APG surfactant in the PURE portfolio.

Coco Glucoside demonstrates exceptional compatibility with all co-surfactant charge types — anionic, cationic, amphoteric and non-ionic — without the charge selectivity restrictions of some surfactant categories, making it ideal for complex 2-in-1 systems where cationic conditioning polymers must coexist with the primary surfactant.

Compatibility Note — Cationic Systems

Unlike anionic surfactants, non-ionic APGs carry no charge and therefore do not undergo the electrostatic precipitation and inactivation that occurs when anionic surfactants contact cationic conditioning polymers such as polyquaternium-10 or guar hydroxypropyltrimonium chloride. GlucoPure CO can serve as the primary surfactant in 2-in-1 cleanse-and-condition formulas at use concentrations where anionic-based systems would require complex charge-balancing or sequential deposition systems.

Product Overview

GlucoPure C10 is Decyl Glucoside — the single C10 chain APG with a characteristically different foam profile versus mixed-chain coco glucoside. The defined single decyl chain delivers denser, creamier, more structurally stable lather with consistent foam kinetics across a wider temperature range than broader-chain APGs. The uniform chain length also provides more predictable rheology in semi-solid and solid formulation systems.

GlucoPure C10 is the preferred APG for premium facial cleansing bars, solid shampoo formats and high-end liquid facial cleansers where foam texture and lather density are primary organoleptic quality differentiators. In solid bar systems, the combination of GlucoPure C10 with IsoPure SCI 85% constitutes the technical backbone of high-performance natural certified syndet shampoo bars.

Foam Quality — Chain Length Effect

The superior foam texture of C10 vs. C8/C10 blends arises from the single-chain uniformity: a defined CMC and predictable micellar packing geometry produces rod-micelles with consistent dimensions under shear. Broader-chain blends produce a more polydisperse micellar population, generating foam with less uniform bubble size distribution and lower foam stability at elevated temperatures. This chain-uniformity advantage is most apparent in solid and semi-solid formulation systems where rheological consistency through temperature cycling is a manufacturing requirement.

Product Overview

BetaPure is Cocamidopropyl Betaine (CAPB) at 35% active concentration in low-DMAPA specification — the most widely used amphoteric surfactant in personal care globally. Its zwitterionic charge character enables simultaneous function across four critical roles in cleansing formulations: foam booster, foam stabiliser, viscosity builder and irritation reducer.

Irritation Reduction Mechanism (Mixed Micelle Formation)

The anti-irritant mechanism of CAPB is precisely characterised in surfactant literature: CAPB molecules incorporate into the micellar shell of anionic surfactants such as SLES and SLS, increasing the effective aggregate size and — critically — reducing the free monomer concentration of the anionic surfactant remaining in solution. It is the monomeric (non-micellar) anionic surfactant that penetrates the stratum corneum and elicits inflammatory response. By driving the equilibrium toward mixed micelles, BetaPure reduces the bioavailable fraction of anionic surfactant at the skin interface without compromising the formulation's total surfactant content or foam volume. This mechanism underpins the near-universal use of CAPB in tear-free baby shampoo formulations globally.

Product Overview

IsoPure SCI is Sodium Cocoyl Isethionate — an acyl isethionate ester of coconut fatty acids that represents the state of the art in mild anionic surfactant technology. Structurally distinct from sulphate surfactants: the isethionate group replaces the sulphate moiety, producing an ester with a dramatically different dermatological profile. In HRIPT (Human Repeat Insult Patch Test) studies, SCI demonstrates irritation indices approaching those of pure water — a benchmark that SLS cannot approach at equivalent concentrations.

The 85% powder grade is the primary enabling raw material for solid shampoo bars. SCI powder's waxy, semi-crystalline character at room temperature forms the structural matrix in water-free solid cleansing formulations, providing the physical form integrity that allows solid bars to hold shape through distribution and use while delivering salon-level foam performance when activated with water. The SCI 85% + APG glucoside combination represents the technical benchmark for premium natural certified solid hair cleansers.

Structural Differentiation from Sulphates

SLS and SLES are sulphate esters: the sulphate group connects directly to the fatty alcohol chain via an ether linkage, with no intermediate chain extension. In SCI, the isethionate group (2-hydroxyethane sulphonate) interposes a two-carbon ethylene unit between the sulphonate and the fatty acid ester linkage. This structural extension significantly reduces the amphiphile's ability to penetrate the stratum corneum lipid bilayers, explaining the material difference in clinical irritation data between SCI and SLS at equivalent use concentrations. The ester linkage also increases susceptibility to saponification at high pH — a relevant consideration for formulation pH targeting.

Formulation Guidance

The 80% paste grade is incorporated into liquid cleansing systems at 50–60°C with stirring, contributing primary mild anionic cleansing character. The 85% powder grade is the standard solid bar matrix material: processed at 60–70°C with the other solid bar components, poured or pressed into bar form, and allowed to cool. Typical bar formula: SCI 85% at 40–50% as primary surfactant, GlucoPure C10 at 10%, GlucoPure C12 at 8%, BetaPure paste at 5%, with actives, preservative and fragrance completing the formula.

Product Overview

SulfoPure SLES is Sodium Lauryl Ether Sulfate at 70% active concentration — the most used primary anionic surfactant in personal care globally by manufactured volume. SLES delivers outstanding foam volume, foam density and cleansing efficiency across the full cosmetic pH range, with hard water tolerance through the ethoxylation preventing calcium salt precipitation that limits classical soap performance in high-hardness water supplies.

The critical distinction from SLS is the ethoxylation with 2 moles of ethylene oxide (EO2) prior to sulphation. This modification introduces a polyethylene glycol spacer between the lauryl chain and the sulphate group, substantially reducing the surfactant's penetration through the stratum corneum lipid bilayer and its interaction with skin proteins — the two mechanisms underlying SLES's materially lower irritation potential versus SLS at equivalent or higher active concentrations.

EO2 Modification — Structural Significance

The EO2 modification inserts two polyethylene oxide units between the lauryl hydrophobic chain and the sulphate head group. The effect on skin interaction is twofold: (1) the larger, more hydrophilic head group reduces the partition coefficient of the SLES monomer into skin lipid bilayers versus SLS monomer; and (2) the EO spacer reduces the density of charge interaction between the sulphate and skin proteins such as keratin and collagen. The combined effect is a surfactant that retains >95% of SLS foam and cleansing performance while delivering substantially lower scores across all standard skin irritation test methodologies (HRIPT, EpiDerm, Zein solubilisation, rabbit eye assay).

Product Overview

AmidoPure provides Cocamide DEA (Coconut Oil Diethanolamide, CDEA) in standard and NF (nitrosamine-free) grades. A fatty acid alkanolamide derived from coconut oil fatty acids by amidation with diethanolamine (DEA), CDEA functions as foam booster, foam stabiliser, viscosity builder and emollient skin feel modifier in anionic surfactant cleansing systems.

The NF (nitrosamine-free) grade is the specification required for personal care applications: manufactured to eliminate free DEA below 0.3% to prevent N-nitrosodiethanolamine (NDEA) formation. NDEA can form when conventional CDEA grades containing residual free DEA contact nitrosating agents present in the formula matrix. The NF grade specification eliminates this risk and is aligned with current EU SCCS guidance and best-practice industry standards for CDEA in cosmetics.

Viscosity Building Mechanism (Cosurfactant)

CDEA molecules incorporate into the hydrophobic core of SLES micelles as cosurfactant, increasing the effective hydrophobic volume of each micelle relative to the charged sulphate head group. This packing parameter shift promotes the spontaneous transition from spherical to rod-like (worm-like) micelles. Rod micelles create viscoelastic network structures that produce markedly higher solution viscosity than spherical micelles at equivalent surfactant concentration. At 1–3% CDEA in an SLES base, this mechanism typically delivers equivalent or greater viscosity than 1–2% NaCl salt thickening, while also improving foam creaminess and foam stability through the increased micellar surface rigidity.
