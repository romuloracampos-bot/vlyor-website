The Surfactant Argument

Mild performance is
not a compromise.

For decades, the personal care industry operated on an assumption that high-foam cleansing required irritating anionic sulphate surfactants, and that mildness could only be achieved at the cost of cleansing efficacy. Alkyl Polyglucoside technology demolished this assumption definitively — APG surfactants match or exceed the foam volume and cleansing efficiency of classic anionic systems while delivering substantially lower irritation potential confirmed in HRIPT and in-vitro keratinocyte studies, complete biodegradability and full compatibility with natural and organic certification schemes.

The VLYOR PURE portfolio gives formulators access to the complete biosurfactant toolkit — from the mildest C8/C10 APGs engineered for baby and sensitive skin through to high-efficiency SLES systems for mass-market production — along with the polysorbate solubilisers, betaine amphoteric systems and sodium cocoyl isethionate mild anionic that complete every professional cleansing formula architecture across personal care, premium hair care and natural cosmetic categories.

Non-ionicSolubiliserHLB 16.5

"The most hydrophilic Polysorbate — the universal solubiliser for light fragrances, water-thin systems and transparent toners requiring absolute clarity at low oil loads."

SoluPure 20 provides Polysorbate 20 (Tween 20, POE-20 Sorbitan Monolaurate) — a polyoxyethylene sorbitan ester with HLB 16.5, the highest in the Polysorbate family. This extreme hydrophilicity makes it the preferred solubiliser for the most water-sensitive applications: light fragrances and essential oils in ultra-thin aqueous systems such as face mists, toners and micellar waters where the oil load is below 0.5% and absolute optical clarity is required.

The mechanism is straightforward: Polysorbate 20 forms small mixed micelles around the oil-soluble ingredient, maintaining it in true solution rather than as a macroscopic droplet. Typical use ratio is 3:1 to 5:1 (PS-20 : oil) for transparent systems. Polysorbate 20 is universally approved in all major regulatory frameworks with an extensive safety record in both rinse-off and leave-on applications.

BetaPure

Cocamidopropyl Betaine 35%

AmphotericFoam StabiliserAnti-IrritantViscosity Builder

"The amphoteric that does everything — simultaneous foam stabilisation, viscosity building, irritation reduction and pH buffering in every cleansing system it enters."

BetaPure is Cocamidopropyl Betaine (CAPB) at 35% active concentration — the most widely used amphoteric surfactant in personal care globally. Its zwitterionic charge character (bearing both positive and negative charges simultaneously at cosmetic pH) enables CAPB to function across multiple roles in the same formula: foam booster, foam stabiliser, viscosity builder and — critically — irritation reducer in combination with anionic primary surfactants.

The irritation-reducing mechanism is well-documented in surfactant science: CAPB forms mixed micelles with anionic surfactants (SLES, SLS), increasing the effective micelle size and crucially reducing the free monomer concentration of the anionic surfactant in solution. It is this monomeric anionic fraction — not the micellar fraction — that penetrates the skin barrier and causes irritation. By suppressing monomer concentration, CAPB dramatically reduces the irritation potential of the combined system while preserving foam performance. This mechanism underpins the near-universal use of CAPB in baby shampoos, tear-free products and sensitive skin cleansers globally.

SulfoPure SLES provides Sodium Lauryl Ether Sulfate at 70% active concentration — the most used primary anionic surfactant in personal care globally by volume, forming the cleansing backbone of the majority of commercial shampoos, body washes and liquid soaps. SLES delivers outstanding foam volume, foam density and cleansing efficiency across a wide pH range, with hard-water tolerance — via ethoxylation preventing calcium and magnesium salt precipitation — that classic soap cannot match.

The critical distinction between SLES and SLS is the ethoxylation (EO2) of the lauryl chain prior to sulphation: the two inserted ethylene oxide units significantly reduce the penetration of surfactant monomer through the skin barrier, resulting in materially lower irritation and eye-sting potential versus SLS, while fully preserving the cleansing and foaming performance. The SLES + BetaPure combination remains the most formulated cleansing system in the world — the universal backbone of mass-market personal care production across all geographies and product categories.

VLYOR PURE — Biosurfactants

AmidoPure

Cocamide DEA

Non-ionicFoam BoosterViscosity BuilderNF Grade Available

"The cost-effective viscosity and foam system — CDEA thickens SLES formulas through mixed micelle formation with no additional thickener required, delivering the dense-foam consumer experience at minimum formulation cost."

AmidoPure provides Cocamide DEA (Coconut Oil Diethanolamide, CDEA) in standard and NF (nitrosamine-free) grades — a fatty acid alkanolamide derived from coconut oil that functions as foam booster, foam stabiliser, viscosity builder and emollient in anionic surfactant systems. The NF (nitrosamine-free) grade is strongly recommended for all personal care applications: manufactured without free DEA to eliminate the risk of N-nitrosodiethanolamine (NDEA) formation, which can occur in conventional CDEA grades when trace nitrosating agents are present in the formula matrix.

The SLES + CDEA viscosity system remains one of the most cost-effective thickening mechanisms in liquid cleansing formulation: typically 1–3% CDEA in an SLES base (without added NaCl) provides significant viscosity increase through cosurfactant-induced micelle elongation, eliminating or substantially reducing the salt thickener requirement and producing a richer foam character. The combination also contributes a mild emollient, skin-feel benefit not present in salt-thickened SLES alone.

Performance Detail

AmidoPure is a semi-solid amber paste at room temperature that requires melting or dispersion in the hot phase of manufacturing. Incorporation into SLES-based formulas is achieved by adding at 60°C with stirring. The viscosity-building mechanism is cosurfactant-driven: CDEA incorporates into SLES micelles, promoting rod-micelle growth and increasing solution viscosity without the electrolyte effects of NaCl thickening. This produces viscosity that is more temperature-stable and less sensitive to dilution than salt-thickened systems.
