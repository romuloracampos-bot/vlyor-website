The Cleansing Argument

Mild performance is
not a compromise.

For decades, the personal care industry operated on an assumption that high-foam cleansing required irritating anionic surfactants, and that mildness could only be achieved at the cost of performance. APG surfactants proved this wrong definitively — Alkyl Polyglucosides match or exceed the foam volume and cleansing efficiency of classic anionic systems while delivering dramatically lower irritation potential, complete biodegradability and full compatibility with natural certification schemes.

VLYOR PURE gives formulators access to the complete biosurfactant toolkit — from the mildest C8/C10 APGs for baby care through to the industrial-scale SLES for high-foam mass-market applications — plus the polysorbate solubilisers, betaine amphoteric systems and SCI mild anionic that complete every professional cleansing formula architecture.

GlucoPure C810 Series

Caprylyl/Capryl Glucoside

APG C8/C10BiosourcedMultiple Grades

"The cleanest C8/C10 APG available — maximum mildness with genuine biodegradability."

GlucoPure C810 Series provides Caprylyl/Capryl Glucoside — the shortest-chain APG (Alkyl Polyglucoside) in the PURE portfolio, produced by the condensation of glucose with a C8/C10 fatty alcohol blend. APGs are biosourced non-ionic surfactants derived entirely from renewable feedstocks — glucose from starch and fatty alcohols from coconut or palm kernel oil — with a biodegradation profile that meets OECD 301B readily biodegradable criteria.

The C8/C10 chain length positions GlucoPure C810 as the mildest APG in the portfolio, with very low irritation potential confirmed by HRIPT (Human Repeat Insult Patch Test) and in-vitro keratinocyte studies. It produces fine, stable foam with excellent rinse-off behaviour and a clean, refreshing skin feel. Available in multiple grades (HSO810, HS215, HS225N) with varying APE content and cloud point — allowing formulators to select the precise technical specification for their application.

SoluPure Series

Polysorbate 20 · 40 · 60 · 80

EmulsifierSolubiliser4 Grades Available

"The Polysorbate family — the global standard for essential oil and fragrance solubilisation in aqueous systems."

SoluPure Series provides the complete Polysorbate (Tween) range in 4 grades (20, 40, 60 and 80) — polyoxyethylene sorbitan fatty acid esters that are among the most used cosmetic emulsifiers and solubilisers globally. Their exceptionally high HLB values (14.5–16.5) make them ideal for solubilising small amounts of oil-phase ingredients (fragrances, essential oils, fat-soluble vitamins, UV filters) into aqueous systems — enabling transparent toners, mists and gels containing oil-soluble actives.

SoluPure 20 (Polysorbate 20, HLB 16.5) is the most hydrophilic grade, ideal for light fragrances and essential oils in water-thin systems. SoluPure 80 (Polysorbate 80, HLB 15.0) provides slightly stronger emulsification for more oleophilic actives. SoluPure 40 and 60 cover intermediate applications. All four grades are COSMOS-eligible, low-irritancy and compatible with both anionic and cationic systems.

BetaPure

Cocamidopropyl Betaine 35%

AmphotericFoam StabiliserAnti-Irritant

"The amphoteric workhorse — viscosity, foam stability and irritation reduction in every cleansing system."

BetaPure is Cocamidopropyl Betaine at 35% active concentration — the most widely used amphoteric surfactant in personal care. Its zwitterionic character (positive at low pH, negative at high pH, neutral at physiological pH) enables it to function simultaneously as a foam booster, foam stabiliser, viscosity builder and irritation reducer in combination with anionic surfactants.

The irritation-reducing mechanism is well-documented: in anionic surfactant systems, CAPB forms mixed micelles with the anionic primary surfactant, increasing micelle size and reducing the fraction of monomeric anionic surfactant — the species responsible for skin and eye irritation. This is why CAPB is near-universal in baby shampoos, tear-free products and sensitive skin cleansers, where it enables high-foam performance while remaining within dermatologically acceptable irritation limits.

IsoPure SCI

Sodium Cocoyl Isethionate

Mild AnionicSolid-form AvailableSulphate-free

"The mild anionic that enabled the sulphate-free revolution — structuring agent for solid cleansing formats."

IsoPure SCI provides Sodium Cocoyl Isethionate in 80% paste and 85% powder grades — the benchmark mild anionic surfactant for solid cleansing bars, syndet (synthetic detergent) bars and premium shampoo bars. SCI is an isethionate ester — structurally distinct from sulphates — with a skin compatibility profile dramatically superior to SLS while delivering comparable foam volume and cleansing efficacy.

The solid-form (85% powder grade) is the primary raw material enabling the "solid shampoo bar" format that has driven significant market growth in sustainable hair care — providing the structural matrix that allows water-free hair cleansing products to hold their form while delivering salon-level foam and rinse-off performance. IsoPure SCI is sulphate-free, parabens-free and COSMOS-eligible, meeting the formulation requirements of the largest natural and organic certification schemes.

SulfoPure SLES

Sodium Lauryl Ether Sulfate 70%

Primary AnionicHigh Performance

"The global benchmark anionic surfactant — unmatched foam density and cleansing power."

SulfoPure SLES is Sodium Lauryl Ether Sulfate at 70% active concentration — the most used primary anionic surfactant in personal care globally. SLES delivers outstanding foam volume, foam density and cleansing efficiency across a wide formulation pH range and performs effectively in hard water — properties that have made it the standard primary surfactant in shampoos, body washes and liquid soaps for decades.

SLES is distinguished from SLS (sodium lauryl sulfate) by the ethoxylation (EO2) of the lauryl chain: this modification significantly reduces the penetration of surfactant monomer through the skin barrier, resulting in substantially lower irritation and eye-sting potential compared to SLS, while preserving the cleansing and foaming performance that makes anionic surfactants commercially dominant.

Sodium Lauryl Sulfate

"Two concentrations for every manufacturing context — the classic anionic in liquid and powder form."

SulfoPure SLS provides Sodium Lauryl Sulfate in two grades: 30% liquid solution and 95% powder — covering liquid manufacturing and solid/powder formulation contexts respectively. SLS remains the most studied surfactant in cosmetic science, with decades of safety and efficacy data, and is still widely used in toothpaste, shampoos and body washes where its maximum cleansing power and low cost are the primary selection criteria.

The 95% powder grade is the essential raw material for solid toothpaste, dry-mix cleansing powders and foam-building powder sachets — applications where liquid surfactants cannot be used. The 30% liquid grade is suited for straightforward shampoo and body wash manufacturing where SLES is not required.

AmidoPure

Cocamide DEA

Non-ionicFoam BoosterViscosity Builder

"The classic foam booster and thickener — cost-effective viscosity and foam enhancement in any anionic system."

AmidoPure provides Cocamide DEA (Coconut Oil Diethanolamide) in standard and NF (nitrosamine-free) grades — a fatty acid alkanolamide that functions as a foam booster, foam stabiliser, thickener and emollient in anionic surfactant systems. The NF (nitrosamine-free) grade is recommended for all personal care applications — it is manufactured without free DEA and prevents N-nitrosodiethanolamine (NDEA) formation that can occur in conventional CDEA grades in the presence of nitrosating agents.

The combination of SLES + CDEA remains one of the most cost-effective shampoo thickening systems available — typically 1–3% CDEA in an SLES base provides significant viscosity increase through mixed-micelle formation, reducing or eliminating the need for salt thickening and providing a creamy foam character.
