The Technical Argument

The invisible ingredient
that defines everything.

In premium sun care, the UV filter system is not a commodity decision — it is the central technical argument of the product. It determines SPF, UVA-PF, sensory profile, stability, compatibility and ultimately whether the formula performs after six months on a shelf in Bogotá at 35°C.

Every major cosmetic manufacturer faces the same formulation challenge: achieving high SPF with low filter load, maximum photostability and an elegant skin feel. These three demands are technically contradictory. Resolving them is the science of UV filter architecture — and precisely what VLYOR SHIELD was built for.

VLYOR SHIELD — UV-A Filter / Photostabiliser

Shield-DHHB

Diethylamino Hydroxybenzoyl Hexyl Benzoate

UV-A FilterPhotostabiliserSelf-Photostable

"The most efficient UVA filter available — and the molecule that rescues every avobenzone formulation."

Shield-DHHB is the gold-standard UVA-I filter for next-generation sun care. Its absorption maximum at 354 nm places it precisely at the peak of UVA-I — the portion of the solar spectrum most responsible for photoageing, indirect DNA damage and melanoma risk, yet least addressed by classical filter systems built around UVB-dominant actives.

What makes Shield-DHHB commercially exceptional is its dual functionality: it delivers outstanding UVA absorption in its own right, while simultaneously acting as a molecular photostabiliser for avobenzone (Shield-AVB), quenching the triplet-excited state that would otherwise cause avobenzone to degrade photochemically. This dual role makes Shield-DHHB the architectural cornerstone of every serious high-SPF formulation on the market — premium facial sunscreens, clinical photodamage prevention products and professional sport sun care all rely on this mechanism.

Oil-soluble, compatible with esters, silicones and non-polar solvents, Shield-DHHB integrates cleanly into standard O/W and W/O oil phases without crystallisation or phase separation at commercially relevant loading levels up to 5%. Its viscous liquid form aids processing and enables co-dissolution of solid UV actives.

Performance Highlights

Critical wavelength (>370 nm): Shield-DHHB extends the critical wavelength of filter systems into the UVA-I range, enabling formulations to achieve ISO 24443 broad-spectrum compliance at lower total filter loads compared to classic avobenzone-only UVA systems.

Photostabilisation mechanism: Shield-DHHB quenches the triplet excited state of avobenzone via an energy-transfer mechanism, preventing the irreversible photoisomerisation that degrades avobenzone under UV exposure. A 1:1 molar ratio provides effective stabilisation at practical loading levels.

Sensory profile: As a viscous liquid, Shield-DHHB contributes a slightly emollient character to the oil phase. At doses up to 3%, sensory contribution is neutral; at higher doses it imparts mild lubricity. Compatible with all major cosmetic silicone grades and ester emollients.

VLYOR SHIELD — Broad Spectrum UV-A/B

Shield-BEMT

Bis-Ethylhexyloxyphenol Methoxyphenyl Triazine

Broad Spectrum UV-A/BSelf-Photostable

"The only organic filter that absorbs both UVA and UVB intrinsically — and requires no photostabiliser to maintain it."

Shield-BEMT is the most versatile broad-spectrum organic UV filter commercially available. Its symmetrical triazine molecular architecture delivers simultaneous strong absorption at 310 nm (UVB) and 343 nm (UVA-II/UVA-I) — a dual-peak profile that no other single organic active achieves at comparable efficiency. This is not a compromise: both absorptions are strong and independent, meaning Shield-BEMT provides genuine broad-spectrum protection, not merely edge coverage at one end of the spectrum.

The defining commercial advantage is intrinsic photostability. Unlike avobenzone — which degrades under UV exposure and requires photostabilisation by secondary actives — Shield-BEMT returns to its ground state after photoexcitation without chemical decomposition. This dramatically simplifies formulation architecture: formulators can build a photostable broad-spectrum system using Shield-BEMT as the single primary filter, complemented only by UVB boosters, eliminating the photostabiliser dependency that adds complexity and cost to avobenzone systems.

Its dual-solubility character is unique among organic UV filters: Shield-BEMT is soluble in oil phases and simultaneously dispersible in aqueous systems with appropriate processing, enabling it to function effectively in classic O/W emulsions, anhydrous sticks, waterproof sport sunscreens and certain aqueous gel architectures.

Performance Highlights

Photostability under continuous exposure: Shield-BEMT maintains >95% of initial UV absorption after 8 hours of simulated solar radiation — the highest photostability rating achievable with an organic UV filter. Critical wavelength: As a filter with strong UVA-I absorption, Shield-BEMT consistently contributes to critical wavelengths above 370 nm, supporting ISO 24443 broad-spectrum compliance without requiring additional UVA actives at standard dosing.

Formulation flexibility: Shield-BEMT dissolves in standard cosmetic oil phases (esters, triglycerides, silicones) and can also be dispersed in aqueous phases via hot-process emulsification. This makes it one of the few UV actives that functions across oil-based anhydrous formulas, classic emulsions and water-dominant gel systems.

VLYOR SHIELD — Broad Spectrum · Aqueous Dispersion

Shield-MBBT

Methylene Bis-Benzotriazolyl Tetramethylbutylphenol

Broad Spectrum UV-A/BSelf-PhotostableWater-Dispersible

"Broad-spectrum protection in an aqueous format — the filter of choice for lightweight water-based sunscreen systems."

Shield-MBBT is a micronised polymeric UV filter available as an aqueous dispersion with dual UV-A/B absorption peaks at 305 nm and 360 nm. It is the only commercially available broad-spectrum organic filter in an aqueous delivery system, which makes it uniquely valuable for formulators working in water-dominant systems where oil-phase filter loading must be minimised or where film texture is critical.

Its polymeric high-molecular-weight structure — formed by the micronisation of solid MBBT particles to sub-micron size — means negligible dermal penetration under normal use conditions. This property has become a significant marketing and regulatory advantage in markets with increasing consumer and legislative attention to UV filter safety, making Shield-MBBT the preferred choice for children's sun care formulations, sensitive-skin products and brands communicating "low skin penetration" claims.

Shield-MBBT is intrinsically photostable: its polymeric structure prevents the photoisomerisation pathways that cause molecular UV filters to degrade, meaning the filter system maintains full efficacy throughout the product use period without requiring photostabiliser actives.

VLYOR SHIELD — UV-A I Filter

Shield-AVB

Butyl Methoxydibenzoylmethane (Avobenzone)

UV-A I FilterRequires Photostabilisation

"The most widely used UVA filter in the world — and the most commercially misunderstood."

Shield-AVB (avobenzone) is the global benchmark for UVA-I filtration. Its absorption maximum at 357 nm delivers the deepest UVA-I protection available from a single organic molecule, making it the most used UVA filter in regulated markets worldwide — present in the majority of EU, LATAM and global sun care formulations requiring UVA labelling.

The formulation challenge that Shield-AVB presents is well-documented in the literature: it undergoes photoisomerisation under UV exposure, converting from its UV-absorbing enol form to a non-absorbing keto form that does not revert spontaneously. Without intervention, a system relying solely on Shield-AVB can lose 30–50% of its UVA efficacy within 60–90 minutes of continuous solar exposure — a significant product performance failure that is invisible to the consumer but measurable under standard photostability testing.

The solution is systematic filter architecture: pairing Shield-AVB with Shield-DHHB or Shield-OCT as triplet-energy quenchers that intercept the excited-state avobenzone molecule before photoisomerisation can occur. This system — in various configurations — is the foundation of every serious high-SPF product on the global market. VLYOR SHIELD provides both the avobenzone and all the photostabilisers needed in one portfolio.

Critical Formulation Requirement

Photostabilisation Is Mandatory

Shield-AVB must always be formulated with a photostabiliser. Use Shield-DHHB (preferred for premium formulations) or Shield-OCT (for waterproof systems). UV-B FilterSelf-Photostable

"The highest molar absorptivity of any approved UVB filter — exceptional SPF contribution at a fraction of the load."

Shield-EHT delivers the most efficient UVB absorption per gram of any approved organic UV filter. Its triazine-based molecular structure — three cinnamate chromophores arranged around a central triazine ring — achieves a molar absorptivity coefficient that exceeds every classical UVB active by a significant margin. This photostability, combined with exceptional molar absorptivity, makes Shield-EHT the preferred UVB efficiency booster in premium high-SPF products where total filter load must be controlled for sensory and cost reasons.

In practice

50–80%
UV-A protection lost without photostabilisation

An unstabilised Avobenzone formula degrades 50–80% of its UV-A absorbing capacity after 2 hours of sun exposure. The difference between Shield-AVB formulated with DHHB and Avobenzone without a photostabiliser is not technical — it's the "SPF 50+ all day" claim you can or cannot put on the label.

1 filter
BEMT covers both UV-A and UV-B

A single BEMT molecule at 5% can replace a two-filter UV-A/UV-B system — reducing total formula complexity, cutting ingredient count on the label, and lowering the risk of filter-filter incompatibilities that destabilise emulsions. Fewer ingredients. Same protection. Better label story.

2027
EU 2027 — The reformulation window is defined.

EU REACH 2024/1328 restricts D5 to ≤0.1% in all cosmetics from June 2027. Brands currently using D5-based sun care formulations have a finite window to reformulate. SilkDuo 503 — our D5-free alternative — is available today. Reformulation support included.
