Why UV Filters Matter

The invisible ingredient
that defines everything.

In premium sun care, the UV filter system is not a commodity decision — it is the central technical argument of the product. It determines SPF, UVA-PF, sensory profile, stability, compatibility and ultimately whether the formula performs after six months on a shelf in Bogotá at 35°C.

Every major cosmetic house — from Belcorp's sun care portfolio to clinical pharmacy brands — faces the same formulation problem: achieving high SPF with low filter load, maximum photostability and an elegant skin feel. These three demands are technically contradictory. Resolving them is the art of UV filter architecture.

"The most effective sunscreen is the one that stays effective — through heat, sweat and light. Photostability is not a feature. It is the only specification that matters after the first hour."

Shield-DHHB

Diethylamino Hydroxybenzoyl Hexyl Benzoate

● UV-A Filter ● Photostabilizer

"The most efficient UVA filter available — and the molecule that rescues every avobenzone formulation."

Shield-DHHB is the gold standard for UVA-I protection in next-generation sun care. Its absorption maximum at 354 nm places it precisely at the peak of UVA-I, where cumulative skin damage accumulates despite causing no immediate visible burn — the most clinically significant portion of the solar spectrum for photoageing and melanoma risk.

What distinguishes Shield-DHHB commercially is its dual function: it delivers exceptional UVA absorption in its own right, and simultaneously acts as a molecular photostabiliser for avobenzone (Shield-AVB), reversing the photodegradation that makes pure avobenzone systems unreliable after three hours of sun exposure. Premium SPF 50+ formulations rarely use one without the other.

Oil-soluble and highly compatible with organic solvents and silicone fluids, Shield-DHHB integrates into oil phases, anhydrous formulas and waterproof systems without phase separation or crystallisation at standard loading levels.

Shield-BEMT

Bis-Ethylhexyloxyphenol Methoxyphenyl Triazine

● Broad Spectrum UV-A/B ● Self-Photostable

"The only filter that covers both UV-A and UV-B — and needs no stabiliser to do it."

Shield-BEMT is the most versatile broad-spectrum filter in the VLYOR SHIELD portfolio and arguably in the global cosmetic sunscreen market. Its triazine molecular architecture delivers simultaneous absorption at 310 nm (UVB) and 343 nm (UVA) — a dual peak that no other single organic filter achieves at comparable efficiency.

The defining commercial advantage of Shield-BEMT is intrinsic photostability. Unlike avobenzone, which degrades under UV exposure and must be stabilised with complementary actives, Shield-BEMT returns to its ground state after excitation without chemical decomposition. This simplifies formulation architecture significantly and reduces total filter load — a critical factor for skin feel and cost management in high-SPF products.

Its dual lipophilic/hydrophilic character is unique: Shield-BEMT is soluble in both oil phases and in water-dilutable systems, giving formulators flexibility that most organic filters cannot offer. It performs in classic O/W emulsions, anhydrous sunscreen sticks and waterproof sport formulations equally well.

Shield-AVB

Butyl Methoxydibenzoylmethane (Avobenzone)

● UV-A I Filter

"The most widely used UVA filter in the world — and the most misunderstood."

Shield-AVB is the global benchmark for UVA-I filtration. Its absorption maximum at 357 nm provides the deepest UVA-I protection achievable with a single organic molecule, making it the most used UVA filter across regulated markets worldwide — from EU Directive 76/768 to the majority of LATAM sun care labels.

The formulation challenge Shield-AVB presents is well documented: it degrades under UV exposure, losing up to 50% of its UVA efficacy within 60–90 minutes without photostabilisation. The solution is systematic filter architecture — pairing Shield-AVB with Shield-DHHB or Shield-OCT, which act as triplet energy quenchers that prevent photoexcited avobenzone from degrading. This system is the foundation of every serious high-SPF product globally.

Shield-AVB is listed under ANVISA, INVIMA and all major LATAM regulatory frameworks at up to 5%, making it the logical first-choice UVA filter for brands entering or scaling in South America.

Shield-OCT

Octocrylene

● UV-B Filter ● Photostabilizer

"The workhorse that keeps your UVA filter alive."

Shield-OCT is the most versatile UVB filter in the Shield portfolio. It absorbs efficiently at 303 nm, contributing meaningfully to SPF. But its primary commercial value lies in its second function: acting as a triplet-state quencher for avobenzone, absorbing the excited-state energy that would otherwise cause Shield-AVB to degrade under solar exposure.

This dual role makes Shield-OCT a near-universal component in high-SPF formulations — it contributes to SPF while simultaneously protecting the UVA filter that provides critical sun damage prevention. Its liquid form at room temperature also acts as a co-solvent for other solid UV filters, reducing the total number of processing steps and enabling higher filter loading without crystallisation.

Shield-EHT

Ethylhexyl Triazone

● UV-B Filter

"The highest UVB molar absorptivity of any approved filter — extraordinary SPF contribution at minimal load."

Shield-EHT delivers the most efficient UVB absorption per gram of any organic filter — its molar absorptivity coefficient exceeds that of all classical UVB actives. This means formulators can achieve equivalent SPF values at significantly lower concentrations, freeing capacity for UVA filters, sensory ingredients or cost reduction without compromising protection.

It is completely photostable, oil-soluble and compatible with the full range of Shield UV-A filters. Particularly valuable in high-SPF minimalist formulations where total filter load must be kept below 15% for sensory and regulatory reasons.

Shield-MBBT

Methylene Bis-Benzotriazolyl Tetramethylbutylphenol

● UV-A/B Broad Spectrum

"Broad-spectrum protection in an aqueous dispersion — the filter of choice for lightweight, water-based formulas."

Shield-MBBT is a micronised polymer dispersion with dual UV-A/B absorption peaks — the only commercially available broad-spectrum filter available in an aqueous format. This makes it the ideal choice for water-based serums, gel sunscreens and spray emulsions where oil-soluble filter loading is limited.

Its polymeric, high-molecular-weight structure means negligible skin penetration — a significant regulatory and marketing advantage in markets with growing concern around filter bioaccumulation. Completely photostable.

Shield-PBSA

Phenylbenzimidazole Sulfonic Acid (Ensulizole)

● UV-B · Water-Soluble

"The only water-soluble UVB filter — for clear, aqueous SPF formulations."

Shield-PBSA is the sole water-soluble UVB organic filter approved globally. This unique property makes it indispensable for clear aqueous gels, hydrating tonics with SPF and any formulation where oil-phase filter loading must be minimised.

Its water solubility also enables formulators to build SPF without adding to the oil phase — critical for very lightweight moisturisers, after-sun sprays and scalp SPF products where a greasy feel is unacceptable. Add to aqueous phase at pH 7–8 (neutralise with NaOH or TEA).
