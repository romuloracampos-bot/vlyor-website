Product Overview

SilkFluid 100 is 100% active polydimethylsiloxane at 100 cSt viscosity — the lightest standard cosmetic-grade dimethicone fluid. At this molecular weight, the silicone chain is short enough to behave with near-water fluidity in handling, while retaining the complete suite of silicone performance properties: extraordinary lubricity, surface tension of ~20 mN/m, breathable semi-occlusive film formation and exceptional thermal stability. This combination makes it the primary emollient ingredient for formulation types where fast, non-greasy application experience is non-negotiable.

The 100 cSt grade is the standard carrier for UV filter and pigment dispersion — its low viscosity ensures homogeneous mixing of crystalline and waxy UV filter actives (AVB, BEMT, EHT) and cosmetic pigments into the oil phase before emulsification, preventing agglomeration and ensuring uniform active distribution throughout the finished product. Additive quantities as small as 0.1% produce measurable reductions in formula soaping and tack.

Mechanism — Lubricity, Film Formation and Sensory Profile

PDMS derives its extraordinary lubricity from the unique geometry of the siloxane backbone. The Si-O bond length (1.64 Å) and bond angle (143°) are significantly larger than C-C equivalents, creating a polymer backbone that is freely rotating and highly flexible. This rotational freedom keeps the non-polar methyl groups (–CH₃) constantly presented at the outer surface regardless of the polymer's conformation on skin, creating a permanently hydrophobic, low-friction surface. At 100 cSt, the polymer chains are short enough that this dynamic methyl presentation produces the fastest lateral spreading rate of the PDMS range — the fluid self-levels across the skin surface in milliseconds, distributing actives uniformly before any evaporation or absorption occurs. The semi-occlusive film formed on evaporation reduces TEWL by ~20–30% (measured by TEWL meters) without the complete occlusion of petrolatum, maintaining skin's natural gas exchange while reducing moisture loss.

Product Overview

SilkFluid 350 is 100% active polydimethylsiloxane at 350 cSt — equivalent to the benchmark Dow DOWSIL 200 Fluid 350 cSt (XIAMETER PMX-200 350 cSt), the most commercially specified dimethicone viscosity grade in personal care globally. At 350 cSt, the PDMS molecular weight produces an ideal balance: the fluid is light enough to spread comfortably and uniformly across skin without requiring significant application force, while being viscous enough to provide a richer, more substantive emollient character than the 100 cSt grade — delivering a satisfying skin feel that consumers read as moisturising and protective.

Beyond emolliency, SilkFluid 350 is the standard dimethicone for formula de-tackification. Many cosmetic formulations contain ingredients that leave a sticky or tacky after-feel — carbomers, certain film-forming polymers, humectants at high concentrations. Adding 2–5% SilkFluid 350 dramatically reduces this tack without altering pH, active concentration or formulation stability. It is also the primary silicone for de-soaping creams and lotions — the characteristic soap-like after-feel of personal care products containing anionic surfactants is eliminated by SilkFluid 350's interfacial activity.

Mechanism — De-Tackification + Balanced Emolliency

At 350 cSt, SilkFluid interacts with skin through a combination of viscous spreading and surface adsorption. The polymer chains partially align along the skin surface, presenting methyl groups outward and creating a low-friction, low-surface-energy layer that physically reduces the tackiness of hydrophilic polymers beneath. This de-tackification mechanism is primarily physical — silicone intercalates between tacky polymer chains and skin, preventing their direct adhesion — and operates at very low concentrations (0.5–2%). The balanced emolliency at 350 cSt produces a skin feel that panellists consistently rate as "moisturised but not greasy" — the optimal sensory positioning for broad-market skin care and sun care products. Thermally processable at up to 200°C with no degradation — compatible with all standard cosmetic emulsification temperatures.

Product Overview

SilkFluid 1000 is 100% active polydimethylsiloxane at 1000 cSt. At this viscosity, the PDMS chains are substantially longer than the 350 cSt grade, producing a more viscous fluid with distinctly different formulation properties: higher substantivity (the polymer film persists longer on the substrate), greater film coherence (longer chains produce a more continuous, coherent film), and a richer, slower-spreading sensory profile that consumers associate with intensive conditioning and protective treatment products.

In hair care, SilkFluid 1000 is the preferred dimethicone for leave-on treatments targeting damaged, coarse or high-porosity hair types — common in the Brazilian, Colombian and Peruvian markets where chemical relaxing, bleaching and Brazilian Blowout treatments are prevalent. The more substantive silicone film provides better combing friction reduction between wash cycles, better heat protection during blow-drying and better frizz control in high-humidity LATAM conditions versus lighter viscosity grades.

Mechanism — Substantivity and Film Durability

Substantivity increases with molecular weight in PDMS fluids because longer polymer chains have more points of interaction with substrate surfaces, increasing the total adsorption energy per molecule. At 1000 cSt, the PDMS chains are long enough to adopt a partially coiled conformation on the hair surface, with multiple siloxane units interacting with the substrate simultaneously. This multi-point adsorption is significantly more resistant to wash-off than the single-contact-point adsorption of shorter-chain 100–350 cSt grades. The result is a conditioning film that survives multiple wash cycles better than lighter silicone grades — measurable in repeat-use combing friction studies as a maintained friction reduction over 3–5 wash cycles versus 1–2 cycles for 350 cSt. In skin care, the more coherent film provides enhanced barrier function, making SilkFluid 1000 the preferred grade for intensive night treatment formulations where extended moisture retention is the therapeutic goal.

Product Overview

SilkFluid 12500 is 100% active polydimethylsiloxane at 12500 cSt — equivalent to the Dow XIAMETER PMX-200 12500 cSt benchmark grade. At this viscosity, the PDMS chains are long enough to contribute substantially to formulation structure — not just emolliency. SilkFluid 12500 imparts viscosity, body and a controlled, slow-spreading character to formulations at concentrations where lower-viscosity grades would be invisible in texture terms. This structural contribution makes it the essential ingredient in high-viscosity hair serums, where the silicone itself provides the product body alongside its conditioning function.

The long PDMS chains at 12500 cSt also provide measurable split-end smoothing: the viscous polymer bridges between mechanically separated hair fibre ends, coating and temporarily fusing the splayed cuticle with a coherent silicone film. The result — visible reduction in the ragged appearance of split ends and a measurable improvement in hair fibre cohesion under light — is the primary commercial claim of premium hair treatment serums globally, and SilkFluid 12500 is the silicone grade that delivers it.

Mechanism — Film Depth, Split-End Bridging and Viscosity Contribution

At 12500 cSt, the weight-average molecular weight of the PDMS is approximately 90,000–100,000 g/mol. Chains of this length adopt a partially entangled, coiled conformation in the bulk fluid. When spread onto hair fibres, they partially unfold and orient along the fibre axis, creating a film of measurably greater thickness and coherence than lower-MW grades. This thicker film provides superior mechanical protection during styling (measurable as reduced combing force and breakage rate) and enhanced water resistance (the thick hydrophobic film is more resistant to humidity-driven swelling of the hair cortex). Split-end bridging: at the mechanically damaged tips of split-end fibres, the viscous, tacky character of 12500 cSt silicone enables capillary wetting of the narrow gap between separated fibre ends, followed by coalescence of the silicone film across the gap as the product is spread along the hair. The result is a temporary but visually significant cosmetic repair of the split-end appearance — the basis of "split-end repair serum" product claims.

Product Overview

SilkVeil is Cyclopentasiloxane (Decamethylcyclopentasiloxane, D5) — the benchmark volatile silicone for cosmetic application. Its cyclic pentameric siloxane structure creates a compound with a boiling point of ~210°C but measurable evaporation rate at skin temperature (37°C) — the characteristic that produces the dry-down, non-greasy finish of modern personal care. SilkVeil evaporates from the skin surface within seconds to minutes of application depending on film thickness and ambient temperature, leaving no residual silicone film beyond the materials it was carrying.

As a precision delivery carrier, SilkVeil's water-like low viscosity (~3.9 cSt) enables exceptionally rapid and uniform spreading of dissolved actives, UV filters, pigments and non-volatile silicones across the skin surface. During the evaporation phase, these materials are concentrated and deposited precisely where they are needed. This delivery mechanism produces a fundamentally better active distribution profile than non-volatile carriers — and the dry finish after evaporation creates the product experience that drives repurchase in LATAM cosmetic categories where skin feel is the primary sensory driver.

Mechanism — Evaporative Delivery + Dry-Touch Finish

D5 evaporates through a diffusion-driven process at skin temperature. The low surface tension (~18 mN/m) and water-like viscosity enable the compound to spread rapidly across the skin surface before significant evaporation occurs, ensuring complete coverage. As evaporation proceeds, the dissolved active cargo is progressively concentrated in the thinning film until the D5 has completely departed, leaving only the deposited materials. This two-stage spreading-then-evaporation mechanism ensures that every region of applied skin receives an equal deposit of active — far superior uniformity than viscous non-volatile carriers that pool in skin depressions. The final dry skin feel arises from the complete absence of residual silicone film — unlike non-volatile emollients that remain on skin continuously, D5-based systems leave skin dry, breathable and without the greasy or sticky character of organic oil-based carriers.

Regulatory Status — EU REACH 2024/1328 (Critical Update)

EU Restriction — Effective June 2027

EU REACH Regulation 2024/1328 (published May 2024) restricts Cyclopentasiloxane (D5) to ≤0.1% in all rinse-off and leave-on cosmetic products sold in the EU market, effective June 2027. D5 cannot function as a carrier at concentrations below 0.1% — any EU market cosmetic currently using D5 will need reformulation before this date. LATAM markets (Brazil, Colombia, Argentina, Peru, Mexico) have no equivalent restriction under current frameworks — D5 remains fully permitted at functional concentrations. VLYOR SILK provides reformulation support for EU-market clients including alternative volatile and semi-volatile carrier systems. Product Overview

SilkDuo 1501 is approximately 15% of an ultra-high molecular weight Dimethiconol silicone gum dissolved in Cyclopentasiloxane (D5) carrier — equivalent to the benchmark Dow XIAMETER PMX-1501 Fluid. Dimethiconol is the hydroxyl end-capped form of polydimethylsiloxane (INCI: HO-PDMS-OH); at ultra-high molecular weight it is a viscoelastic solid (silicone gum) rather than a fluid. The D5 carrier acts as a solvent for the gum, producing a pourable, processable liquid that delivers ultra-HMW silicone gum performance within standard cosmetic manufacturing processes.

The two-stage delivery mechanism is SilkDuo 1501's defining formulation behaviour. During application, D5 enables rapid, uniform spreading of the dissolved gum across the skin or hair surface. As D5 evaporates — within seconds to minutes at skin temperature — the gum is progressively concentrated on the substrate surface and deposited as a coherent, substantive film. The resulting film is significantly more durable than any fluid silicone grade: the ultra-HMW polymer chains provide multi-point substrate adsorption that resists wash-off, and the hydroxyl end-groups of dimethiconol provide additional polar surface affinity beyond the non-polar PDMS backbone.

Mechanism — Gum Film Deposition and Substantivity

The substantivity of silicone on hair increases exponentially with molecular weight because longer polymer chains have exponentially more potential adsorption contact points per molecule. At ultra-HMW gum range (estimated >500,000 cSt equivalent), the dimethiconol molecule in SilkDuo 1501 has hundreds to thousands of siloxane units capable of interacting with the hair surface. Once partially adsorbed, the remaining unadsorbed chain segments continue to collapse onto the surface under Van der Waals forces, eventually producing a flat, tightly adsorbed polymer conformation that is mechanically difficult to remove by water or mild shampoo. The hydroxyl end-groups (–OH) of dimethiconol provide additional interaction with the polar sites of the hair cuticle (exposed amino acid carboxylates and amines) that methyl-terminated dimethicone cannot access — contributing to the superior wash-off resistance observed in gum blend formulations versus fluid silicone formulations at equivalent loading. The durable conditioning film deposited from SilkDuo 1501 provides: combing friction reduction that persists across multiple wash cycles; heat protection through thermal barrier insulation; split-end cosmetic repair by film-bridging of separated fibre ends; and anti-frizz by hydrophobic barrier that prevents atmospheric moisture from entering the hair cortex and causing cuticle swelling.

Product Overview

SilkDuo 1401 is approximately 13% of an ultra-high molecular weight Dimethiconol gum in a blend of Cyclotetrasiloxane (D4) and Cyclopentasiloxane (D5) — equivalent to the Dow DOWSIL 1401 Fluid. The specific D4/D5 carrier ratio was selected to balance two formulation requirements: preventing product freezing (D4 alone can solidify at ambient temperature due to its higher melting point) and providing a mobile, pourable consistency for cosmetic processing — while delivering the dual evaporation profile that is SilkDuo 1401's primary commercial differentiator.

The bi-phase evaporation mechanism creates a progressive sensory transition that distinguishes SilkDuo 1401 from single-carrier gum blends. D4, with lower boiling point than D5, begins evaporating rapidly upon application — providing an immediate dry-down sensation and initial active deposition. D5 follows at a slower rate, extending the application window for the gum to distribute fully before deposition. The final dimethiconol gum film deposited by SilkDuo 1401 shows measurably superior water resistance and substantivity compared to SilkDuo 1501 — attributed to the more complex carrier composition creating optimal film morphology during deposition.

Mechanism — Bi-Phase Evaporation and Water-Resistant Film Formation

The D4/D5 carrier blend in SilkDuo 1401 produces a two-stage evaporation profile because D4 (boiling point ~175°C) evaporates faster from a surface film than D5 (boiling point ~210°C) at the same temperature. As D4 preferentially evaporates, the remaining film becomes increasingly D5-rich, with the gum concentration rising progressively throughout the evaporation period. This progressive concentration increase creates an extended "open time" during which the viscosity of the depositing film increases gradually — enabling the gum chains more time to spread, relax and maximise their contact with the substrate surface before the film solidifies. The result is a more tightly adsorbed, more cohesive gum film with greater resistance to water penetration and mechanical removal versus films deposited from a single-carrier system. Mechanism — Optical Enhancement via Refractive Index and Phenyl Aromaticity

Light interaction with surfaces is governed by the refractive index difference between the material and its surrounding medium. At the air/hair surface interface, a higher refractive index material reflects more incident light specularly (mirror-like reflection) and diffusely (surface scattering). The phenyl groups on SilkLume's backbone, with their conjugated π-electron systems, create local regions of high polarisability that increase the molecular refractive index from the PDMS baseline of 1.40 to approximately 1.46. When SilkLume coats the hair cuticle, this creates an optical interface with measurably higher reflectance across the visible spectrum, perceived by observers as enhanced gloss and brightness. SPF mechanism: the higher refractive index of the silicone phase surrounding TiO₂ or ZnO particles modifies the effective optical path length through the particle, increasing the scattering cross-section for UV radiation per unit mass of filter — effectively making each gram of inorganic filter work harder. Studies on DOWSIL 556 equivalents consistently demonstrate 10–20% SPF improvement in mineral filter formulations versus equivalent PDMS vehicles. Oxidation stability: phenyl groups on silicones are significantly more resistant to thermal and UV oxidation than methyl groups — the aromatic ring system provides radical stabilisation that methyl carbons cannot. SilkLume-containing formulations maintain their optical performance over shelf life better than PDMS-only systems.
