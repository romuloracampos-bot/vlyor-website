The Performance Argument

Active ingredients that
earn their label claim.

The cosmetic active market is saturated with ingredients that perform in vitro and disappoint in-formula. The difference between a bioactive that delivers and one that does not comes down to three variables: purity, stability and delivery concentration. An ascorbic acid at 90% assay does not behave the same as one at 99.7%. A hyaluronic acid outside its molecular weight specification does not film-form as claimed. These are not edge cases — they are the routine quality failures of commodity active supply.

VLYOR VITAL was built on a single principle: every active in the portfolio must perform to its specification, every time, at the concentration stated in the TDS. This means verified assay on each batch, real molecular weight data on hyaluronic acid grades, and full characterisation documentation — not a supplier specification sheet rebranded under a private label.

"An efficacy claim is only as strong as the active behind it. The formulator who understands this selects ingredients differently — and their products perform differently in the market."

PanthoActiv 98

D-Panthenol (98.0% Minimum Assay)

Vitamin B5D-Form · High PuritySkin & Hair

"The most broadly used conditioning active in cosmetics — at the purity level that actually delivers on every claim."

PanthoActiv 98 is VLYOR's premium-grade D-Panthenol at a minimum assay of 98.0% — the biologically active enantiomer of panthenol. Once applied to skin or hair, it is enzymatically converted to pantothenic acid (Vitamin B5), a key component in the synthesis of Coenzyme A and essential to cellular metabolism, keratinocyte proliferation and tissue repair.

In cosmetic application, PanthoActiv 98 delivers three well-established mechanisms simultaneously: humectancy (hygroscopic properties that bind moisture in the stratum corneum), skin barrier support (stimulates keratinocyte differentiation to reinforce the cornified envelope and reduce TEWL), and anti-inflammatory activity (reduces cytokine-mediated irritation responses, particularly relevant in post-procedure and sensitive-skin formulations). In hair applications, it penetrates the cuticle and cortex, improves tensile strength, reduces breakage under mechanical stress and imparts softness and shine.

The 98% assay specification matters commercially: D-Panthenol at 85–90% assay — standard in lower-cost supply chains — contains significant proportions of inactive L-panthenol and process impurities that dilute efficacy and can cause formulation instability at acidic pH. At 98%, dosing is precise, results are predictable, and the efficacy published in the clinical literature is reproducible.

PanthoActiv DL

Vitamin B5Racemic · Volume Grade

"The cost-optimised Vitamin B5 — for high-volume formulations where performance at scale is the brief."

PanthoActiv DL is the racemic (DL) form of panthenol — an equal mixture of D- and L-panthenol enantiomers. Only the D-form is biologically active, meaning PanthoActiv DL delivers approximately 50% of the active dose per gram compared to PanthoActiv 98. This makes it appropriate for high-volume rinse-off formulations — shampoos, body washes, conditioners — where panthenol's sensory contribution (slip, softness, manageability) is the primary objective and cost efficiency is critical.

PanthoActiv DL is the global volume standard for panthenol. The majority of commercial shampoos, conditioners and body lotions in the LATAM market use DL-panthenol at 0.5–1.0% for cost-effective conditioning claims. The choice between DL and D-grade is a commercial and technical decision: for leave-on treatments, serums and products making specific skin repair, barrier function or wound-healing claims, PanthoActiv 98 is the correct specification. For volume rinse-off conditioning, PanthoActiv DL delivers equivalent sensory results at half the active-ingredient cost.

VLYOR VITAL — Vitamin E

TocoActiv E

Tocopheryl Acetate

Vitamin EAntioxidantLipid-Stabiliser

"The oil-phase antioxidant that protects the formula and the skin simultaneously."

TocoActiv E (tocopheryl acetate) is the most commercially used form of Vitamin E in cosmetic formulation — the acetate ester of alpha-tocopherol that provides superior oxidative stability compared to free tocopherol while delivering identical biological efficacy once de-esterified by skin esterases upon application. The esterification serves a dual commercial purpose: it stabilises the ingredient itself against air oxidation during manufacturing and shelf life, and it protects lipid-containing formulas from rancidity and peroxide formation by acting as a chain-breaking antioxidant in the oil phase.

Biologically, TocoActiv E is converted to alpha-tocopherol in the stratum corneum and dermis, where it integrates into cell membrane phospholipid bilayers and neutralises lipid peroxyl radicals before they propagate oxidative damage. This mechanism provides direct protection against UV-induced lipid peroxidation, reduces photoageing markers and supports the skin's natural antioxidant network. At ≥97.0% assay, maximum specific antioxidant activity per gram is guaranteed.

RetinActiv A

Retinyl Palmitate 1,700,000 IU/g

Vitamin AAnti-AgeingLight & O₂ Sensitive

"The gentlest retinoid — proven anti-ageing activity with a tolerability profile suitable for every skin type."

RetinActiv A (retinyl palmitate) is the fatty acid ester of retinol and palmitic acid — the most stable and best-tolerated form of Vitamin A in cosmetic use. Enzymatically hydrolysed in the epidermis to retinol and subsequently oxidised to retinoic acid, it activates retinoid nuclear receptors (RAR, RXR), stimulates collagen synthesis via upregulation of COL1A1 and COL3A1, accelerates cellular turnover and reduces hyperpigmentation — the same biological cascade as prescription retinoic acid, at a substantially lower irritation and photosensitivity burden.

Supplied at 1,700,000 IU/g — the internationally recognised concentration specification for cosmetic retinyl palmitate — enabling precise dosing against published clinical protocols and regulatory guidelines. This is the concentrated active, not a premix: formulators retain full control over final concentration and carrier selection.

Stability management is non-negotiable: RetinActiv A is highly sensitive to light, oxygen and heat. It must be incorporated at cool-down (≤40°C), protected by antioxidants (TocoActiv E, AscorActiv C), and packaged in opaque or airless containers to maintain potency throughout shelf life.

VLYOR VITAL — Vitamin C

AscorActiv C

Ascorbic Acid (L-Ascorbic Acid)

Vitamin CAntioxidantBrighteningpH-Sensitive

"The most studied topical antioxidant — and the most demanding to formulate correctly."

AscorActiv C (L-ascorbic acid) is the biologically active form of Vitamin C and the most clinically studied topical antioxidant in cosmetic science. At 10–20%, it delivers measurable collagen synthesis stimulation, reduces UV-induced pigmentation via tyrosinase inhibition, neutralises reactive oxygen species in the epidermis, and contributes to the skin's photoprotective capacity — independently of UV filter systems.

The formulation challenge is well-established: L-ascorbic acid is inherently unstable at neutral pH and in the presence of water, oxidising progressively to dehydroascorbic acid and diketogulonic acid — compounds with no biological activity that cause characteristic product yellowing. Stable, efficacious formulations require pH below 3.5, low water activity or anhydrous environment, antioxidant co-actives and appropriate packaging. At ≥99.7% assay, AscorActiv C ensures maximum active content per gram and minimises the oxidation impurities that accelerate degradation cascades.

Clinical Performance Highlights

Collagen synthesis: L-ascorbic acid is essential for the hydroxylation of proline and lysine in procollagen synthesis — the rate-limiting step in collagen crosslinking. At 10–20% topical application, published studies demonstrate measurable increases in type I collagen mRNA expression and procollagen synthesis, with reduction in fine line depth after 12 weeks of consistent use.

Brightening mechanism: AscorActiv C inhibits tyrosinase at multiple points in the melanogenesis pathway, reducing new melanin synthesis and the DOPA → dopaquinone conversion. This positions BakuActiv as a clinically validated retinoid-equivalent with a superior tolerability profile — directly addressable to sensitive skin, pregnant consumers and brands communicating natural or clean positioning.

Mandelic Acid

AHAExfoliantBrightening

"The gentlest AHA — large molecular structure, slower penetration and effective exfoliation without the sting."

MandelActiv (mandelic acid) is an aromatic alpha-hydroxy acid derived from bitter almonds with a molecular weight of 152.15 Da — the largest of the commonly used AHAs, compared to glycolic acid at 76 Da. This larger molecular size results in slower, more controlled dermal penetration that translates directly into a gentler skin response with equivalent exfoliation efficacy to lower-molecular-weight AHAs at comparable concentrations. MandelActiv is the preferred AHA for sensitive skin types, darker Fitzpatrick skin tones with post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH) risk, and markets where glycolic acid intolerance or irritation complaints are common.

This makes it particularly valuable in brightening treatments where chemical exfoliation and pigmentation control are simultaneously desired, as the active addresses both the surface accumulation of pigmented cells and the ongoing melanin synthesis.

MalicActiv (L-malic acid) is a dicarboxylic alpha-hydroxy acid naturally occurring in apples and other fruits, with a molecular weight of 134.09 Da — larger than glycolic acid (76 Da) and lactic acid (90 Da), resulting in slower dermal penetration and a milder irritation profile. It is commonly used in combination AHA systems alongside glycolic, lactic or mandelic acids, where it broadens the exfoliation activity and the buffering range while reducing total irritation load.

HyalActiv Series is VLYOR's high-molecular-weight sodium hyaluronate at 1.0–1.5 MDa — the grade of hyaluronic acid that delivers the surface-acting hydration, film-forming properties and visible plumping effect associated with premium skin care. Cooling ActiveTRPM8 AgonistAnalgesicFragrance

"The TRPM8 receptor agonist — physiological cooling without temperature change, with proven analgesic and antimicrobial properties."

MenthoActiv (L-menthol) is the principal active component of peppermint oil — a cyclic terpene alcohol with a precise physiological cooling mechanism: direct agonism of the TRPM8 (Transient Receptor Potential Melastatin-8) ion channel in sensory neurons, triggering the same neural signal generated by actual cold temperature in the absence of any temperature change. Beyond cooling, MenthoActiv delivers documented mild analgesic activity (counterirritant via TRPV1 desensitisation, reducing pain perception in muscles and joints), antimicrobial activity (active against Gram-positive bacteria and dermatophytes at concentrations above 0.1%), and provides a characteristic clean, fresh fragrance note that positions it uniquely as a functional aromatic active rather than a passive fragrance ingredient.

In practice

98% vs 50%
PanthoActiv 98 vs DL: the claim you can make

DL-Panthenol delivers ~50% of the biologically active D-form per gram. PanthoActiv 98 delivers ≥98% D-form. At identical formulation concentrations, the effective active dose is nearly double — and the product can claim "D-Panthenol" on the label rather than the generic "Panthenol". That distinction matters in premium positioning.

0.1%
Hyaluronic acid: less is more, but only at the right grade

HyalActiv at 1.0–1.5 MDa delivers measurable surface hydration at 0.1% — one tenth of a gram per 100 g formula. At that loading, the cost per unit is negligible, but the clinical claim and consumer perception of "hyaluronic acid" on the label drive purchase decisions worth multiples of the ingredient cost.

Open formulation.
BakuActiv: efficacy without the constraints of retinol

BakuActiv (Bakuchiol) provides retinol-comparable gene expression data without the photosensitivity, pregnancy restriction or stability challenges of retinol. In markets where retinol communication is restricted or where consumer sensitivity to retinol limits market reach, Bakuchiol opens formulation space that retinol closes.
